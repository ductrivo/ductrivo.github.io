### High temperatures: what are the employer's obligations?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/6f08a955-0d31-4b95-9760-621c0835c145/464x261_sc_chaleur-entreprises.jpg"
    alt="High temperatures: what are the employer's obligations?"
    caption="Fortes chaleurs : quelles sont les obligations pour les employeurs ?" %}


High temperatures can pose health risks to workers. The French Labor Code specifies the employer's obligations in the event of a heatwave.

Employers must provide thermal protection equipment such as light clothing, hats, and sunglasses. They must also ensure that employees have access to water so that they can cool down regularly.

In addition, employers may be required to reorganize work by avoiding physically demanding tasks during the hottest hours of the day. They must also ensure that employees are informed of the risks associated with high temperatures and the measures taken to protect them.

When the temperature exceeds 30°C, employers are required to conduct a thermal risk assessment and take appropriate measures to protect their employees. If the temperature exceeds 35°C, employers must suspend work for two consecutive hours between 12:00 and 14:00.

Employers who fail to comply with these obligations may be taken to court and fined. Employees also have the right to report violations of the rules by their employer to the Labor Inspectorate.

### Roland Garros: The statistics and records of precocity of Moïse Kouamé, the 17-year-old winner of his first Grand Slam match


The Roland-Garros tournament saw the exceptional victory of 17-year-old Moïse Kouamé in his first Grand Slam match. Kouamé, the youngest player to win a match at this level, defeated Italian Flavio Cobolli in five sets (6-4, 3-6, 6-2, 6-7, 6-3).

Before Roland-Garros, Kouamé had already made a name for himself by winning the Australian Open junior doubles tournament and reaching the semi-finals in singles. He is also ranked 12th in the junior world rankings.

In addition to his age, Kouamé also broke another record at Roland-Garros: he is the first player on the French Davis Cup team since Henri Cochet in 1937 to win a Grand Slam match before the age of 18.

This victory also marks a first for France, as no French player had ever won their first match at Roland-Garros since Gael Monfils in 2004.

Kouamé was remarkably supported by the French spectators during his victory, who praised his aggressive game and competitive spirit. He is now considered one of the players to watch for the future of French tennis.
