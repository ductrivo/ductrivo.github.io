### 'Putting the i's in the i's': how a simple letter changed the French language


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/af7eb759-c73d-48ac-ad38-7e7a3364af18/464x261_sc_gettyimages-1165187422.jpg"
    alt="'Putting the i's in the i's': how a simple letter changed the French language"

Title: "Putting the i's in i": how a simple dot influenced the French language

In medieval manuscripts, some letters looked similar and could easily be confused, making it difficult to understand words. To solve this problem, a simple sign was added by scribes: the "point souscrit". This sign, now called the "point souscrit", allowed for the differentiation of similar letters and thus facilitated reading. This practice spread throughout Europe and left a lasting mark on the French language. Thus, the point souscrit became an important element of our current orthography.
