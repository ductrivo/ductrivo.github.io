### On the route of the 2026 Tour de France: In Carcassonne, our tips for a weekend like in the days of the knights.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/cb3ea932-b318-4b38-9002-541aa4cf18cd/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="On the route of the 2026 Tour de France: In Carcassonne, our tips for a weekend like in the days of the knights."
    caption="Sur la route du Tour de France 2026 : À Carcassonne, nos conseils pour un week-end comme au temps des chevaliers" %}


For the 2026 Tour de France, Carcassonne offers a weekend full of medieval experiences in the Occitanie region. The city has been a UNESCO World Heritage site since 1997, and Alban Forlot, a host on ICI Occitanie, guides us for an immersion in the Middle Ages.

The weekend begins with a visit to the Medieval City, where the ramparts, towers, and houses with slate roofs transport visitors back in time to the age of knights. The narrow, winding streets are lined with artisan shops and traditional restaurants.

The day continues with a visit to the City Museum, which presents the history of Carcassonne from prehistoric times to the present day. The collections include objects found on site, models, and paintings depicting the city over the centuries.

On Sunday morning, it is possible to take a boat for a cruise on the Canal du Midi, which connects the Mediterranean Sea to the Atlantic Ocean. The views of the bridges and houses in Carcassonne are magnificent and offer a different perspective on the city.

The weekend ends with a traditional Occitan meal in a local restaurant, where it is possible to taste specialties such as cassoulet, snails, and rouille. Local wines are also recommended to complement the meal.

In conclusion, Carcassonne offers a weekend full of medieval experiences in a city that is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Visitors can discover the City, the City Museum, and the surrounding area by following the advice of Alban Forlot, a host on ICI Occitanie.
