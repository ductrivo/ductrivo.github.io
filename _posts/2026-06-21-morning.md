### Le Mesnil-Mauger, a typical village of the Pays d'Auge


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/c1d2f4e9-98a4-4692-b4db-5913ec525540/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Le Mesnil-Mauger, a typical village of the Pays d'Auge"
    caption="Le Mesnil-Mauger, un village typique du Pays d'Auge" %}


Le Mesnil-Mauger is a typical village of the Pays d'Auge, located between Mezidon, Saint-Pierre sur Dives, and Cambremer. Today, the village is part of the new commune of Mezidon Vallée d'Auge, and it has experienced remarkable growth in the 19th and 20th centuries.

Le Mesnil-Mauger is a traditionally agricultural village, with fertile land suitable for growing cereals and potatoes. In the 19th century, the village also experienced industrial expansion, with the opening of a cheese factory and a Calvados distillery.

In the 20th century, tourism became an important sector for the village. Le Mesnil-Mauger is located in a region known for its picturesque landscapes and architectural heritage, with timber-framed houses and Romanesque churches. Visitors are attracted by local product markets, music festivals, and art exhibitions.

The village was also marked by World War II. On June 6, 1944, the day of the Normandy landings, the village was occupied by the Allies. It was liberated by American troops and became an important strategic point for the advance of Allied forces towards Paris.

Today, Le Mesnil-Mauger continues to charm visitors with its rural charm and cultural heritage. It offers views of the Normandy countryside and is located near several other picturesque villages in the Pays d'Auge.

### 'This would be a groundbreaking, unprecedented measure': and what if the Île-Saint-Denis bridge reopened without cars?


The redevelopment project for the Île-Saint-Denis bridge proposes the removal of motor vehicle lanes, which would be a unique measure of its kind. This would make the bridge exclusively for pedestrians, cyclists, and public transport. This decision is motivated by the goal of improving the quality of life for residents while promoting environmentally friendly modes of transport.

This measure was announced by the Mayor of Paris, Anne Hidalgo, at the Mobility Paris 2021 event. It is also supported by the Seine-Saint-Denis local authority and environmental protection associations.

The project also includes the creation of green spaces, rest areas for pedestrians and cyclists, as well as facilities for people with disabilities. Work is expected to begin in 2023 and last for about two years.

This measure is considered an important step towards a more sustainable, eco-friendly, and accessible city for residents. It is part of Paris's climate-energy plan, which aims to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and improve air quality.

However, this decision has also drawn criticism, particularly due to the potential impact on traffic and local businesses. Impact studies are currently underway to assess these effects and propose suitable solutions.
