### Tour de France: Stage 1 classification and overall classification with Jonas Vingegaard in yellow


The Tour de France began last Friday with the first stage, an 8.2-kilometer individual time trial in Saint-Sulpice-de-Roy and Plaisir-du-Gatinais. Jonas Vingegaard, rider for the Jumbo-Visma team, won the first stage in 9 minutes and 4 seconds, taking the lead in the general classification and wearing the yellow jersey.

In the general classification, Vingegaard is followed by Tadej Pogačar, winner of the 2020 Tour de France, at 13 seconds, and Geraint Thomas, winner of the 2018 Tour de France, at 45 seconds.

In the points classification, Mark Cavendish, rider for the Deceuninck-Quick Step team, is in the lead with 50 points, followed by Fabio Jakobsen and Jasper Philipsen at 38 points each.

The Tour de France continues until July 24 with a total of 21 stages, including six mountain stages and a final arrival in Paris on the Champs-Élysées.
