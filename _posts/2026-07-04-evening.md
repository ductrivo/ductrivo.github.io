### Two fires in the Gard region, 115 hectares burned in the Drôme… Firefighters continue to battle the fires.


In recent weeks, there has been a significant increase in wildfires in France, particularly in the regions of Gard and Drôme. The total number of fires has reached two, with an area of 115 hectares affected in the Drôme region. Firefighters are still battling these fires, which were triggered by unfavorable weather conditions and an increased risk of fire due to the persistent drought.

Local authorities have implemented preventative measures to reduce the risk of fire spread, including a ban on campfires and the mobilization of volunteers to monitor sensitive areas. Firefighters have also received reinforcements to strengthen their ability to combat fires.

Authorities have called for adherence to preventative measures to avoid any risk of fire spread, and have encouraged citizens to stay informed about the local situation by following weather reports and information from emergency services. Firefighters have also emphasized the importance of not putting themselves in danger when confronted with a fire, and of calling emergency services immediately if necessary.

Wildfires can have serious consequences for human health, the environment, property, and the local economy. Therefore, authorities have implemented an action plan to reduce the risk of wildfires and to protect citizens and their property.

### 'The Death Road' in Ukraine: how Kyiv is targeting Russian convoys


The title "The Road of Death" refers to a strategic road in eastern Ukraine, where Ukrainian forces have set up roadblocks and ambushes to prevent Russian convoys from reaching areas controlled by pro-Russian separatists.

The convoys are primarily composed of military vehicles, weapons, and ammunition, as well as civilians being evacuated. Ukrainian forces have claimed to have blocked or destroyed several convoys since the beginning of the conflict in 2014.

Russian authorities have accused Ukrainians of attacking humanitarian convoys, but international observers have reported that these convoys also contained weapons and military personnel.

The conflict has already caused the deaths of more than 13,000 people and has displaced over a million people. Negotiations to end the conflict have been repeatedly interrupted, but fighting continues regularly in certain areas of the Donbass.

The United States and the European Union have imposed economic sanctions on Russia due to its support for pro-Russian separatists, but these measures have not led to a resolution of the conflict.

The conflict has also had an impact on relations between Ukraine and Russia, which have been strained since the Orange Revolution in 2004. The two countries remain in disagreement on several issues, including the status of Crimea, which was annexed by Russia in 2014.

The conflict in eastern Ukraine continues to be a point of contention between the two countries and poses a threat to peace and stability in the region.
