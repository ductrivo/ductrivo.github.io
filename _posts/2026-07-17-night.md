Experience a magical moment by immersing yourself in an authentic village, meticulously recreated over thirty years, which illustrates life in France between the 1900s and 1970s. This family of artisans has succeeded in preserving the spirit of this era, offering visitors an immersive and authentic experience.

The village includes over sixty original or reconstructed buildings, each presenting a different aspect of daily life in France during that period. You can visit traditional shops, a school, a church, a farm, and even a prison.

The artisans, dressed in period costumes, will warmly welcome you and invite you to participate in the daily activities of the village. You can watch demonstrations of weaving, pottery, carpentry, blacksmithing, and other traditional crafts.

The village also offers a wide variety of live performances, including concerts of traditional French music, folk dances, and theatrical productions. You can also attend demonstrations of traditional games, such as pétanque or chess.

The village is located in a picturesque natural area, offering visitors an idyllic setting for their stay. There are also several traditional restaurants and hotels nearby, allowing visitors to spend several immersive days in this bygone era.

In summary, this recreated village offers an immersive and authentic experience of life in France between the 1900s and 1970s. Visitors can participate in the daily activities of the village, attend live performances, and discover traditional crafts. The village is located in a picturesque natural area and also offers traditional accommodations for visitors who wish to spend several immersive days in this bygone era.

### TOURIST INFORMATION - Which landing sites in Normandy are candidates for inclusion in the UNESCO World Heritage List?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/a5dfbac9-b33b-41ed-96dc-a60b71f18e1d/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="TOURIST INFORMATION - Which landing sites in Normandy are candidates for inclusion in the UNESCO World Heritage List?"
    caption="CARTE - Quels sont les sites du Débarquement en Normandie candidats au classement au patrimoine mondial de l'Unesco ?" %}


The D-Day landing sites in Normandy, which are candidates for inclusion in the UNESCO World Heritage List, include several beaches where the Allied landing took place in 1944:

* Utah Beach, located in the Manche, west of Cherbourg.
* Omaha Beach, located in the Calvados, east of Bayeux.
* Gold Beach, also located in the Calvados, between Arromanches and Courseulles-sur-Mer.
* Juno Beach, located in Lower Normandy, near Courseulles-sur-Mer.
* Sword Beach, located in the Calvados, west of Caen.

UNESCO must issue its verdict by the end of July 2026 to determine whether these sites will be included in the World Heritage List. These historic beaches were the site of one of the most important events in modern history, namely the Allied landing in Normandy, which marked a turning point in World War II.

### Paris: A memorial plaque honoring a couple of Righteous Among the Nations was defaced during the period of commemorations for the Vel' d'Hiv' roundup.


The memorial dedicated to the Just couple, located in Paris, was vandalized during the period of commemoration of the Vel' d'Hiv' roundup. The couple in question, Gérard and Cécile Goldstein, saved over 100 Jewish children during the German occupation. The memorial, inaugurated in 2015, is located in the Marais district and depicts a Jewish family hidden under a bed. The memorial plaque was stolen and replaced with another one, which did not mention the Goldsteins. This action provoked strong indignation and condemnation from various institutions and individuals. Authorities assured that security measures would be strengthened to protect the memorial.
