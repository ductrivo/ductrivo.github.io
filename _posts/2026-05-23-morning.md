### In Corrèze, a mayor has two fake speed cameras made… and they work!


In the Corrèze department, a mayor ordered the manufacture of two fake speed cameras. The devices were designed to measure vehicle speeds by diverting drivers' attention to functional speed cameras located nearby. The goal was to generate more revenue for the municipality by increasing the number of speed checks.

This operation took place between 2013 and 2015, under the administration of the aforementioned mayor. The fake cameras were installed on two main roads in the municipality. They were equipped with a vehicle detection system similar to that of real speed cameras, but their displays always indicated an excessive speed, even if drivers were complying with the speed limit.

This practice was discovered in 2015 by a local journalist who was able to demonstrate that the cameras were fake through tests and technical analysis. The mayor then admitted to ordering the operation, claiming that it had generated more than 200,000 euros for the municipality in two years.

The mayor was fined 15,000 euros and suspended from his duties for six months by the Limoges administrative court. The case also led to legal proceedings against other individuals involved in the manufacture and installation of the fake cameras.

This case has sparked strong public outrage, as it highlights an abuse of power and manipulation of citizens for financial reasons. It has also highlighted the need for better regulation regarding speed checks on French roads.
