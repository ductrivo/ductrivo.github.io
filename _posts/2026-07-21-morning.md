### Pesticides, water, wolf: what does the emergency agricultural bill adopted by the National Assembly contain?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/76fae1d0-4184-490a-b576-b9df23af2f11/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Pesticides, water, wolf: what does the emergency agricultural bill adopted by the National Assembly contain?"
    caption="Pesticides, eau, loup : ce que contient le projet de loi d'urgence agricole adopté par l'Assemblée nationale" %}


The emergency agricultural bill, recently passed by the National Assembly, includes several relevant measures regarding water and pesticides. This text is the result of an agreement reached between the two chambers.

The provisions regarding pesticides aim to strengthen food safety regulations, particularly to protect consumers from health and environmental risks. Pesticide manufacturers will now be required to provide more detailed information about their products, including their environmental impact. Furthermore, competent authorities will be empowered to temporarily ban the use of certain pesticides in cases where there is a risk to human or environmental health.

Regarding water, the bill includes measures to strengthen the protection of water resources and improve their management. Agricultural operations will be required to reduce their water consumption and implement systems for capturing, storing, and treating wastewater. Furthermore, competent authorities will be empowered to impose sanctions on agricultural operators who do not comply with these measures.

Finally, the bill also includes provisions for the protection of the wolf, particularly in areas where it is considered an endangered species. Hunters will be required to follow certain rules to protect this mammal, including avoiding killing it when it is feeding or protecting its young.

In summary, the emergency agricultural bill aims to strengthen regulations on pesticides and water, as well as the protection of the wolf. Pesticide manufacturers will be required to provide more detailed information about their products, while agricultural operations will be required to reduce their water consumption and implement systems for capturing, storing, and treating wastewater. Furthermore, competent authorities will be empowered to impose sanctions on agricultural operators who do not comply with these measures. Finally, the bill also includes provisions for the protection of the wolf.

## Île-de-France


### At Lake Der, the church of Champaubert tells the story of this village that was submerged.


The Der Lake, located in the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region of France, is home to the Champaubert church, which bears witness to the history of a lost village. Champaubert was a small rural village that was submerged during the creation of the artificial lake in the 1970s. The church, built in the 12th century, is the only visible structure of this lost village. It is located on an island in the middle of the lake and can only be accessed by boat. The church is a remarkable example of Romanesque architecture and attracts thousands of visitors each year who are interested in the local history and culture. It is also used for occasional religious ceremonies. The Der Lake, with its Champaubert church, offers a unique experience and a remarkable historical testimony.

### Monique Barbut met face à face with Sébastien Lecornu to discuss her resignation this Tuesday.


Monique Barbut met at Sébastien Lecornu last Tuesday to discuss her departure from her position. Ms. Barbut, who held the position of Executive Secretary for the United Nations for combating desertification and dry migration, announced her resignation in January 2021. She had been appointed to this position by Ban Ki-moon in 2008 and served until 2021. During her tenure, she had worked to improve the management of natural resources and to combat the effects of climate change in developing countries. She had also been involved in several international initiatives to promote sustainability and ecological resilience. Her departure was followed by a transition period, during which she continued to work with the UN to ensure an effective transfer of responsibilities.
