### Prince Albert II of Monaco follows in the footsteps of an ancestor in the Drôme: 'a romantic hero who emerges from the shadows'


{% include figure.html
    src="https://france3-regions.franceinfo.fr/image/lgbJ7STycP2TxOWAzN8kt3ifd8A/930x620/filters:format(webp)/regions/2026/05/06/69fb17095a347750327875.jpg"
    alt="Prince Albert II of Monaco follows in the footsteps of an ancestor in the Drôme: 'a romantic hero who emerges from the shadows'"
    caption="Le prince Albert II de Monaco sur les traces d'un ancêtre dans la Drôme : 'un héros romanesque qui sort de l'ombre'" %}


Prince Albert II of Monaco made an extraordinary visit to the small village of Bathernay, in the hills of the Drôme, on Wednesday, May 6th. This visit is part of a remarkable connection between the Principality of Monaco and this rural area, linked to the figure of Imbert de Bathernay, a ancestor of the prince. Imbert de Bathernay is described as a romantic hero who emerged from obscurity after recent historical research. He was a medieval knight who played an important role in defending the Principality of Monaco against Saracen attacks in the 12th century. Prince Albert II visited the castle of Bathernay, where Imbert de Bathernay lived and fought, to honor his ancestor and strengthen the ties between the Drôme and Monaco.

### Heatwave: This is historic, France has never experienced a May day as hot as this


Exceptional weather event: France recorded the hottest day in May in its history. On April 30, 2021, the maximum temperature reached 35.2°C in Montélimar (Vaucluse), breaking the previous record of 34.6°C recorded in Saint-Armand (Gironde) in May 1949. This heatwave is due to high atmospheric pressure, which blocks cold weather fronts and allows high temperatures to spread northwards across Europe. Authorities have called for vigilance due to the risk of fires, air pollution, and public health.

*[Source](https://www.leparisien.fr/meteo/vague-de-chaleur-cest-historique-la-france-na-jamais-connu-un-jour-de-mai-aussi-chaud-25-05-2026-S5ROHFA4AVHRNKXX7HZZYEYAGA.php)*

### 'Everyone adapts as best they can': schools are always forced to use makeshift solutions to cope with the heat.


"Everyone adapts as best they can": Schools continue to adhere to the traditional school system to cope with the heat. Safety and comfort measures are implemented in schools to protect students and teachers during the summer months. Schools have also implemented reduced hours to prevent overheating, as well as limited or cancelled outdoor activities. In addition, some schools have installed air conditioning systems to maintain a comfortable temperature in classrooms. School authorities have also encouraged students to wear light clothing and drink plenty of fluids to stay hydrated.
