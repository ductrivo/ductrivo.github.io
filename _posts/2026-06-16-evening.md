### Paris La Défense Arena: New naming contract overshadows business district


The Paris La Défense Arena is a multi-purpose stadium located in La Défense, in the western suburbs of Paris. It was built between 2017 and 2021 and has a capacity of 30,000 seats for sporting events and 41,500 seats for concerts. The stadium is the largest in France and one of the largest in Europe.

On January 28, 2022, a new naming contract was announced for the stadium, which is now called "Accor Arena". This contract lasts 15 years and is estimated at 60 million euros per year. This makes the Paris La Défense Arena the most profitable stadium in France.

The new name replaced that of "Bercy Arena", which was the name of the stadium since its construction in 1984 until 2022. The change of name was accompanied by a renovation of the interior and exterior of the stadium, which includes the creation of a transparent roof to improve the visibility of outdoor events.

The Paris La Défense Arena will host many major events, such as concerts by international stars, football and basketball matches, as well as circus and theater performances. It is also planned to host international sporting competitions, such as the Davis Cup in tennis and the 2023 Men's European Basketball Championship.

The new naming contract has been praised by local and national authorities, who have highlighted the importance of the Paris La Défense Arena for the region and for France. They have also noted that the stadium will contribute to strengthening Paris's position as a leading international city for sporting and cultural events.
