### 'The Lady of Stone' at the Dome de Paris: what is this religious musical about Notre-Dame?


"The Lady of Stone" at the Louvre Cathedral (now known as the Paris Dome) is a religious musical centered on Notre-Dame. Created in 2018, this theatrical production was conceived by French composer Richard Cocciante and librettist Luc Plamondon. The story takes place in Paris during the Middle Ages and follows the life of Pierre, a young sculptor who works on the Notre-Dame cathedral. He falls in love with a statue he has created, the Lady of Stone, and must confront the moral and spiritual challenges that this entails. The musical is a spectacular production with memorable songs, elegant costumes and sets, and a talented cast. It has been praised by critics and audiences for its spiritual content and captivating music.

1. The minor or their legal representative (e.g., a parent) can file a complaint with the local police or the relevant agency in the field of sexual abuse against minors.
2. The police investigate the allegations and identify the suspects. If necessary, they interview witnesses and gather evidence.
3. If the police believe there is sufficient evidence to prosecute the suspect, they refer the case to the competent prosecutor.
4. The prosecutor decides whether there is sufficient evidence to prosecute the suspect in court. If so, they initiate a legal proceeding and order the suspect to appear in court.
5. The court hears the witnesses and examines the evidence before making a decision on the suspect's guilt. If the suspect is found guilty, they may be sentenced to penalties ranging from imprisonment to fines or alternative criminal sanctions.
