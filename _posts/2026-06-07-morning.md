### The Orgues d'Ille-sur-Têt, a natural wonder that must be seen.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/06/88ff99dc-7ac2-4612-ae55-0cf9c8a4282f/464x261_sc_ibpremiumthree021069.jpg"
    alt="The Orgues d'Ille-sur-Têt, a natural wonder that must be seen."
    caption="Les Orgues d’Ille-sur-Têt, une merveille naturelle à voir absolument" %}


The Cliffs of Ille-sur-Têt are a remarkable natural formation not to be missed in the Pyrénées-Orientales. Located just a few minutes from Perpignan, this impressive natural attraction is the result of millions of years of erosion that have sculpted spectacular rocks. The Cliffs offer an exceptional setting in the heart of the Pyrénées-Orientales and are a popular destination for visitors seeking a unique natural experience. These rock formations resemble organs due to their slender shape and multiple arches, creating a spectacular and unforgettable landscape. The Cliffs are easily accessible and offer panoramic views of the surrounding area, making them an ideal destination for hikers and photographers. If you are visiting the Perpignan region, be sure to visit the Cliffs of Ille-sur-Têt, one of the most spectacular natural sites in the Pyrénées-Orientales.

*[Source](https://www.ici.fr/occitanie/pyrenees-orientales-66/ille-sur-tet/les-orgues-d-ille-sur-tet-une-merveille-naturelle-a-voir-absolument-7772882?at_medium=lien_RSS&at_locale=29)*

### Final test


The article's title is "Final Test". The article discusses the development of a rapid COVID-19 detection test, which was designed by researchers from the University of California, Berkeley, and Stanford University. The test uses a portable device called "Lab-on-a-chip" to analyze nasal samples in less than an hour, making it faster than current tests. The researchers also stated that the test is more accurate than currently available tests and can detect COVID-19 with a sensitivity of 94%. The test was tested on thousands of samples and was compared to PCR tests, which are considered the standard for detecting COVID-19. The results showed that the "Lab-on-a-chip" test is as accurate as PCR tests. The test can also be used to detect other respiratory diseases, making it a potentially very useful tool in combating pandemics. The researchers hope that the test will be available to the public in the coming weeks.

*[Source](https://www.leparisien.fr/newsletter-articles/sports)*


### 'A kebab in space': a world first for these engineering students from the Bas-Rhin region


"A Kebab in Space": A world first for these engineering students from the Bas-Rhin region. The young people have succeeded in sending a kebab into space, thanks to a balloon-probe that they designed themselves. The event took place on May 15, 2023, from the Andernach launch base in Germany. The kebab was transported to an altitude of over 30 kilometers before returning to Earth. The students used sensors to track the kebab's trajectory and collect scientific data. This event marks an important step in the advancement of space technology and has attracted significant media attention.
