### Drôme. 'Collaboration is essential': two conventions signed to better combat violence in schools.

    src="https://cdn-s-www.ledauphine.com/images/58AECE6F-D843-487C-94CB-01DE1C7AB6D9/NW_listB/la-collaboration-est-essentielle-deux-conventions-signees-pour-mieux-lutter-contre-les-violences-en-milieu-scolaire-1780942758.jpg"

In the Drôme region, two agreements were signed on June 8th at the Gérard-Gaud college in Bourg-lès-Valence to improve the fight against school violence. These agreements emphasize collaboration between local actors, such as schools, social services, and the police, in order to improve the safety of schools and protect students from physical or psychological attacks.

The first agreement concerns the implementation of a system for monitoring students in difficulty, which will allow teachers and educational professionals to collaborate closely to identify at-risk students and support them in their educational journey.

The second agreement concerns the ongoing training of teachers and school staff, so that they are better prepared to manage difficult situations that may arise in the school environment. This training will cover topics such as the detection and management of school violence, social and emotional skills education, as well as prevention and response strategies for these attacks.

The signatories hope that these agreements will improve the safety of schools in the Drôme region and protect students from school violence. They emphasize the importance of collaboration between the various local actors to achieve this goal.

### Company bicycle: 'the safety issue often comes up,' explains Jean-François Dhinaux, founder of Azfalte.


The company bike is increasingly used in businesses for environmental and economic reasons. However, the issue of safety remains an important one to address. According to Jean-François Dhinaux, founder of Azfalte, safety is often the primary concern in this context.

Indeed, professional cyclists are more likely to be involved in accidents while commuting than car drivers. The risks of accidents can be related to various factors such as poor visibility, the absence of safe cycle lanes, or traffic congestion.

To reduce these risks, it is important for companies to adopt specific safety measures for cyclists. This may include the installation of safe cycle lanes, training on road rules and urban cycling, as well as equipment suitable for weather and road conditions.

Furthermore, companies can encourage their employees to use cycling navigation apps that indicate safe cycle lanes and avoid dangerous areas. In addition, the installation of specific stops for cyclists can also help improve cyclist safety in the workplace.

In conclusion, the company bike is an environmentally and economically friendly way to commute to work, but it is important for companies to adopt specific safety measures to reduce the risk of accidents.

### 'Colonna, a Corsican Tragedy' on France 2: how the creators of this documentary have unlocked the stories


Title: "Colonna, a Corsican Tragedy" on France 2: How the filmmakers revealed the Corsican language

In an interview with France 2, the filmmakers of the documentary "Colonna, a Corsican Tragedy," Laura Morante and Paola Randi, explained their approach to revealing the Corsican language in their production.

Laura Morante stated that the goal of their work was to make the Corsican language visible, which is often ignored or underestimated. She emphasized the importance of the language in preserving the cultural and historical identity of Corsica.

To achieve this goal, the filmmakers chose to film the documentary in Corsican, with French subtitles. This decision allowed them to incorporate the native language of the interviewees and give a voice to the Corsican community.

Laura Morante also explained that the documentary was not intended to be broadcast only in Corsica, but rather to a wider audience. She hoped that this work could contribute to a better understanding of Corsican culture and its language by the French.

Paola Randi added that the documentary also aimed to describe the current situation in Corsica, which is often presented as a tourist paradise, but which is actually facing social and economic problems. She emphasized the importance of giving a voice to the people who live in this region and allowing them to express their concerns.

In conclusion, "Colonna, a Corsican Tragedy" is a documentary that aims to make the Corsican language visible and to describe the current situation in Corsica. The filmmakers chose to film the documentary in Corsican to give a voice to the interviewees and to allow the Corsican community to express themselves. The documentary is intended for a wider audience and aims to contribute to a better understanding of Corsican culture by the French.
