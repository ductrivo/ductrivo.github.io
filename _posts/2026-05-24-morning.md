In May 2023, a heatwave struck France, with high temperatures across the country. This situation is unprecedented so early in the year. Valérie Masson-Delmotte, a member of the High Council for Climate, stated that situations like this will become almost normal in 20 or 30 years. ### Embark on a tour behind the scenes of a unique place in the world: The Familistère de Guise in the Aisne.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/3257d4c0-3200-45db-95c1-51e963d2a6fd/464x261_sc_famlistrere.jpg"
    alt="Embark on a tour behind the scenes of a unique place in the world: The Familistère de Guise in the Aisne."
    caption="Embarquez pour une visite dans les coulisses d’un lieu unique au monde : Le Familistère de Guise dans l’Aisne" %}


Visit the behind-the-scenes of a unique place in the world: The Familistère de Guise, located in the Aisne. Behind its large glass roofs and bright courtyards, this site tells the story of an extraordinary social experiment. Schools, theater, swimming pool, gardens, or collective housing: everything was designed to improve the daily lives of working-class families. Built in 1841 by industrialist Philippe Buchez, the Familistère de Guise is a unique example of utopian architecture and industrial society. It was designed to meet the needs of workers and their families by creating an autonomous community where all necessary services were brought together. The Familistère de Guise is an important witness to 19th-century French social and industrial history, and it has been a UNESCO World Heritage site since 2019.

### When the alarm sounded for the last time: Farewell to the SMN


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/38aa52fc-4d1c-475b-962d-3aedacd653d5/464x261_sc_gettyimages-1419562066.jpg"
    alt="When the alarm sounded for the last time: Farewell to the SMN"
    caption="Quand la sirène a sonné pour la dernière fois : l’adieu à la SMN" %}


The sound of the siren marks the definitive end of the Société Métallurgique de Normandie (SMN) after nearly a century of industrial activity in the Caen agglomeration. The SMN played a crucial role in the industrial heart of the region, employing thousands of people and contributing to the local economy. Its history is marked by the red hues of the sky, the lives of workers, and the collective memory of the city.

Founded in 1920, the SMN experienced rapid growth in the 1950s and 1960s, becoming one of the largest employers in the region. It produced special steels for various industries, including aerospace and automotive. However, with increasing competition from foreign companies and the decline of some traditional industries, the SMN faced growing financial difficulties in the 2000s.

In 2019, the company was acquired by ArcelorMittal, which announced the closure of the plant in 2020. This decision sparked a strong reaction in the region, with protests and calls to save the company. Despite the efforts of unions and local authorities, the closure of the SMN finally came into effect on December 31, 2020, marking the end of an era in the industrial history of Caen.

The closure of the SMN had significant consequences for the region, with job losses and a negative impact on the local economy. However, local authorities also announced plans to reinvest in the industrial site and attract new businesses to Caen. The closure of the SMN therefore also marks a new stage in the history of the Caen agglomeration, with challenges and opportunities ahead.

*[Source](https://www.ici.fr/emissions/histoire-s-et-patrimoine-de-normandie/quand-la-sirene-a-sonne-pour-la-derniere-fois-l-adieu-a-la-smn-1714982?at_medium=lien_RSS&at_locale=29)*

### Accused of proselytizing by some, too expensive for others: the hospital in Tours is eliminating its chaplain positions.


The Tours hospital has announced the elimination of its chaplain positions, sparking controversy. Some view this change as a form of proselytism prohibited in French public institutions, while others consider the positions to be too expensive for the hospital. The chaplains were responsible for providing spiritual support to patients and hospital staff. They have been replaced by lay missionaries, who also offer spiritual assistance but without belonging to a specific religion. This decision was made as part of a comprehensive reorganization of the hospital aimed at reducing costs and improving the efficiency of services offered to patients.
