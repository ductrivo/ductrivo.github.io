The Tour Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes, formerly known as the Critérium du Dauphiné, will take place from Sunday, June 7th to June 14th, 2026. The race starts in Vizille and finishes at the Plateau de Solaison, with a route consisting of eight stages.

The profiles of the stages reveal a varied race, with challenging climbs in the Alps and the Central Massifs. Stages 2 to 4 will be particularly demanding for the riders, with long ascents to the cols of Porte and Glandon.

The queen stage will take place on Thursday, June 10th, with an arrival at the Col de la Madeleine, which reaches an altitude of 2,067 meters. The riders will also have to tackle the cols of Croix-de-Fer and Galibier during this stage.

The timetables for each stage will be published later, but it is planned that the race will start each day at 13:00. Details on the start and finish of each stage will also be available soon.

- Line 8: closed between the stations Balard and Porte de Choisy, from noon to 7 pm.

The Autrand case was a French criminal investigation that took place in 1984. The body of businessman Jean-Louis Autrand was found in his property in Saint-Cyr-l'École, near Paris.
