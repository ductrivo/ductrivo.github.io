### Loire Parliament: The idea of giving the river a voice and rights


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/aed4acb4-1ecd-4ceb-ace7-69ccf977ca65/464x261_sc_1.jpg"
    alt="Loire Parliament: The idea of giving the river a voice and rights"
    caption="Parlement de Loire : l'idée qui veut donner une voix et des droits au fleuve" %}


The Loire Parliament is a unique initiative aimed at giving the Loire River a legal voice and rights. This idea aims to grant the river its own representation to reflect in a new way on the relationships between humans, the territory, and life, taking into account climate change issues. The goal is to create a constructive dialogue between humans and nature in order to improve the sustainable management of the river and its environment. This initiative was launched by a coalition of environmental groups, researchers, and citizens committed to protecting the natural and cultural heritage of the Loire. The Loire Parliament is an innovative example of ecological democracy that aims to give a voice to natural elements in order to protect and preserve them for future generations.

*[Source](https://www.ici.fr/emissions/reflets-de-loire-ici-orleans/parlement-de-loire-l-idee-qui-veut-donner-une-voix-et-des-droits-au-fleuve-9941555?at_medium=lien_RSS&at_locale=29)*

### A ten-meter-long whale found dead on a beach on the island of Île de Ré


A ten-meter-long whale was found dead on a beach on the island of Île de Ré. Local authorities have confirmed the death of this marine animal, but the cause of death has not yet been determined. Marine biologists are being called in to study the whale's body and identify the possible causes of its death. The whale is a protected species in France, and its death has attracted great interest from local and national media. Authorities have asked visitors to respect the area and not approach the whale's body to allow the experts to do their work.

### 'Un unacceptable level of unrest, but no major damage: what lessons can be drawn from the PSG victory celebrations?'


Celebrations of Paris Saint-Germain's (PSG) victory were marked by "unacceptable excesses," but without major damage, according to authorities. Hundreds of supporters invaded the Place de l'Étoile in Paris after PSG's victory in the Champions League on Sunday, May 29th. Law enforcement used tear gas to disperse the crowds. Some supporters were injured and several arrests were made. The Mayor of Paris, Anne Hidalgo, said that these events were "unacceptable" and called for better management of future celebrations. Authorities also emphasized that most supporters had been respectful and that there had been no major damage. PSG won its first Champions League title after defeating Bayern Munich in the final.
