### Lyon: A blind man dies after an accidental fall on the subway tracks.


On February 1st, in Lyon, a blind man died after an accidental fall on the subway tracks. The circumstances of the accident are still under investigation. The 52-year-old man was accompanied by a person who was able to move away before the impact. The police and emergency services were immediately alerted and attempted to save him, but he died on the spot. The subway services temporarily suspended their operations to allow investigators to investigate the accident. The police have called on witnesses to come forward to provide additional information. The identity of the victim has not been revealed, but it is known that he was a resident of Lyon and that his visual impairment had not prevented him from leading an active life. The subway services have assured their cooperation in the investigation and have offered their condolences to the victim's family.

### 'Selfies and 'offensive remarks': How a photo involving Donald Trump and Giorgia Meloni triggered a diplomatic crisis'


On July 25th, Italian Foreign Minister Giorgia Meloni posted a selfie with US President Donald Trump on Twitter during a meeting at the US Embassy in Rome. In the photo, the two politicians were smiling and giving a boxing victory sign. However, an offensive comment was added to the tweet by an anonymous user, comparing Meloni to a prostitute.

This post triggered a diplomatic crisis between the US and Italy. Italian authorities expressed their disappointment with Trump's behavior, who did not condemn the offensive remarks. In response, the White House stated that Trump could not control the comments of Twitter users.

Finally, Minister Meloni removed the tweet and apologized for not taking the offensive remarks seriously. However, she also criticized the Italian press for exaggerating the importance of the incident.

This diplomatic crisis highlights the fragility of relations between the US and Italy, as well as the difficulty of managing offensive comments on social media.

Shein, the Asian fast-fashion giant, has ended its partnership with BHV Marais after only seven months of collaboration. This alliance has been described as a strategic misstep due to several factors.

1. The presence of Shein within BHV Marais, a historic Parisian department store, has drawn criticism against the brand for its fast-fashion practices and negative environmental impact.
2. Sales were lower than initially expected, resulting in financial losses for BHV Marais.
3. The collaboration has also been criticized by some BHV Marais customers, who have expressed their disappointment with the Shein brand and its product quality.
4. Shein's sales at BHV Marais were also affected by restrictions related to the Covid-19 pandemic, which led to a decline in sales in the fashion sector.
5. Finally, it was reported that Shein had filed a collective dismissal request for 200 BHV Marais employees, which sparked a strong reaction from unions and customers.

As a result, the shopping center will be sold to a new operator, thus ending this unfortunate collaboration between Shein and BHV Marais.
