### The Loire River, heavily dried up near Ancenis


The Loire, a major river in France, has a significantly dried-up bed near Ancenis. This situation is due to a prolonged drought that has affected the region. Local authorities have taken measures to monitor and manage the consequences of this phenomenon, including implementing a low-water alert plan. Discussions are also underway with the French Ministry of the Environment to assess the potential impacts on biodiversity and threatened aquatic species. Local farmers have also been affected by this situation, as the drop in water levels has led to a decrease in irrigation needed for their crops.

*[Source](https://www.leparisien.fr/video/actualites/meteo-climat/le-lit-de-la-loire-fortement-asseche-pres-dancenis-11-07-2026-AG7HHMFJINDWLOV6VRKDIYBOX4.php)*


### Horse theft and masked break-in at a car dealership: a man declared unfit in Essonne


A man was declared mentally unfit by the Essonne court for committing two serious offenses: the theft of a pony and the masked break-in of a car dealership. The incidents occurred in Épinay-sur-Orge, Essonne. On March 23, the man stole a 10-year-old pony belonging to a local owner. He was identified through surveillance footage and arrested by the police on the same day.

On April 28, he committed a masked break-in at a car dealership located in Épinay-sur-Orge. He stole several car parts and was arrested on the spot by the police.

On April 29, he was tried for these two offenses and declared mentally unfit due to mental illness. He was placed under judicial supervision for six months and prohibited from approaching the locations where the incidents occurred.

### [1/2] Milivoj Milosavljevic, 'the parking lot attacker'


Milivoj Milosavljevic, known as the "parking strangler," is a Serbian serial killer who committed murders between 1998 and 2003. He was convicted of seven murders and several sexual assaults in the Belgrade area of Serbia.

Milosavljevic chose his victims from among women who were leaving parking lots or garages in Belgrade. He lured them with a white car, drove them outside the city, and sexually assaulted them before strangling them.

The first victims of Milosavljevic were identified in 1998, but he was not arrested until 2003 after witnesses recognized his car. He confessed to his crimes and was sentenced to seven life sentences for the murder of his victims.

Milosavljevic is currently in prison in Serbia, where he is still considered a dangerous sexual offender. His conviction has been praised by the families of the victims and by Serbian authorities, who have emphasized the importance of combating organized crime and sexual violence.

*[Source](https://www.leparisien.fr/podcasts/crime-story/12-milivoj-milosavljevic-letrangleur-des-parkings-11-07-2026-BVZGJUVTXBDITO5ERRFUIOFIGU.php)*
