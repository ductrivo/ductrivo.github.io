### The sinking of the Leopoldville (Episode 1): The 'Titanic' of World War II


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/97374137-9b2c-478a-94a7-dc6c572558d4/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="The sinking of the Leopoldville (Episode 1): The 'Titanic' of World War II"
    caption="Naufrage du Léopoldville (épisode 1) : le 'Titanic' militaire de la Seconde Guerre mondiale" %}


The sinking of the troopship Leopoldville, nicknamed the "military Titanic" of World War II, is a tragic event that occurred in late 1944. Allied troops retreat in the face of the Nazi offensive and call for help from troops based in England. However, the Leopoldville will not follow the planned scenario. This drama is told by Benoît Martin, a journalist at ICI Cotentin, in this two-part episode.

On December 5, 1944, the Leopoldville leaves England with over 7,000 American and Canadian soldiers on board. The ship is escorted by British destroyers. However, on December 23, while heading towards France, it is attacked by a German submarine. The ship is hit several times and begins to sink rapidly.

The soldiers are trapped in the ship's holds, which do not have escape hatches. The survivors must fight to reach the surface and breathe fresh air. Unfortunately, over 700 soldiers lose their lives in this disaster.

This event is considered one of the greatest maritime disasters of World War II. The reasons for this attack are still debated, but it is clear that the sinking of the Leopoldville had tragic consequences for the soldiers and their families.

### Nanny platform Nounou-top ordered to remove misleading guarantees


The online childcare platform, Nounou-top, is facing the cancellation of its guarantees, according to a recent decision by the French Ministry of Justice. Authorities have found that some of the guarantees provided by the platform were misleading or inaccurate, leading to problems for parents and accusations of fraud.

The Nounou-top platform offers an online childcare service that allows parents to find part-time or full-time childcare providers. The guarantees provided by the platform were intended to assure users that the childcare providers recruited were vetted and qualified, but some of these guarantees have been questioned.

Authorities have also found that the platform had not complied with some of the French regulations regarding online childcare services, particularly those that require thorough vetting of childcare providers and regular monitoring of their quality.

Nounou-top has responded to these accusations by stating that it is ready to cooperate with authorities to resolve the identified issues and improve its service. It has also announced measures to strengthen the vetting of childcare providers and ensure users have greater security.

This decision by the Ministry of Justice is a major setback for the Nounou-top platform, which had already faced similar accusations in the past. However, it remains popular with many parents in France, who appreciate its easy access and flexibility.
