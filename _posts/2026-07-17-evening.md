### Do you know the 'ideal paradise' described by the author of The Three Musketeers?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://france3-regions.franceinfo.fr/image/zEXktVS3YjSEWWDGXfjSVU4s0eM/930x620/filters:format(webp)/regions/2026/03/12/conte-monte-cristo-v2-69b2b832d110c057975887.png"
    alt="Do you know the 'ideal paradise' described by the author of The Three Musketeers?"
    caption="Connaissez-vous le 'paradis terrestre' de l'auteur des Trois Mousquetaires ?" %}


Do you know the "earthly paradise" of the author of The Three Musketeers? The series "En vol" invites you throughout the summer to guess the names of jewels of Ile-de-France heritage. Today, we went to Port-Marly in the Yvelines to discover a castle built by a famous author.

The castle in question is the Château de la Bussière, known as the "earthly paradise" of Alexandre Dumas. It was built between 1840 and 1843 to serve as a residence for the writer during the summers. The castle is a remarkable example of neo-Gothic architecture, with its towers, arched windows, and grand stone staircase.

The castle's park is also remarkable, with its French gardens, tree-lined paths, and views of the Seine. The castle has been used as a backdrop for several films and television series, including Richard Lester's 1973 adaptation of "The Three Musketeers".

The Château de la Bussière is open to the public during the summer season and offers guided tours to discover the history of the castle and its famous owner. It is also possible to stroll through the park and enjoy its natural beauty.
