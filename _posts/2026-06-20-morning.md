### Putting mulch in your garden, yes, but why?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/1022ebbd-caac-4873-8552-e5addc5ca02c/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Putting mulch in your garden, yes, but why?"
    caption="Mettre du paillage dans son jardin, oui, mais pourquoi ?" %}


Mulching is a method of improving soil that involves covering the soil in gardens or terraces with an organic or inorganic material. This practice has several advantages: it reduces evapotranspiration, prevents weed growth, and protects plant roots from winter frost.

It is recommended to use mulching in your garden to improve soil quality, reduce water evaporation, and prevent weed growth. The choice of material will depend on your garden: pine or oak wood for acidic plants, compost or dead leaves for neutral and alkaline plants.

It is important not to make the mulch too thick, as this can prevent air and water from penetrating the soil. It is also important to avoid putting mulch around flowering or fruiting plants, as this can prevent them from growing properly.

The disadvantages of mulching are few: it can be expensive if you use an inorganic material, and it can also attract harmful insects if it is not properly maintained.

In conclusion, mulching is a useful practice for improving the quality of your garden soil, reducing water evaporation, and preventing weed growth. It is important to choose a material that is suitable for your plants and to avoid making the mulch too thick to allow air and water to penetrate the soil.
