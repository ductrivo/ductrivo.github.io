### February 4, 1981 - Professor Philippe Maupas, the man behind the hepatitis B vaccine, dies.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/87dfcd33-e3e5-40db-a7ab-75551bbf68ba/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="February 4, 1981 - Professor Philippe Maupas, the man behind the hepatitis B vaccine, dies."
    caption="4 février 1981 - Le professeur Philippe Maupas, l'homme derrière le vaccin contre l'hépatite B, trouve la mort" %}


February 4, 1981 marks the tragic death of Professor Philippe Maupas, the inventor of the hepatitis B vaccine. This loss is highlighted in the program "Histoire en Touraine," presented by Cédric Delaunay, a history-geography teacher at the Descartes high school in Tours. Maupas, originally from the Touraine region, dedicated his life to scientific research and made a major contribution to public health by developing an effective vaccine against hepatitis B. His death, which resulted from a car accident, is considered a significant loss for the scientific and medical community.

### Environment. Heatwave: which departments have the highest number of electric kettles?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://cdn-s-www.ledauphine.com/images/7DAAB557-7DB4-46A0-844E-815EC58D6887/NW_listB/quels-sont-les-departements-qui-comptent-le-plus-de-bouilloires-thermiques-1781886515.jpg"
    alt="Environment. Heatwave: which departments have the highest number of electric kettles?"
    caption="Environnement. Canicule : quels sont les départements qui comptent le plus de bouilloires thermiques ?" %}


During the current heatwave in France, a study revealed that 9 out of 10 homes are not suitable for extreme heat. The departments most affected by this problem are those with the highest number of electric kettles: Loiret, Essonne, Seine-et-Marne, Yvelines, and Hauts-de-Seine. New homes or those that have been insulated as part of an energy performance assessment (DPE) are also affected by this problem. This means that the majority of French people have homes that do not allow them to effectively protect themselves from extreme heat, even when they live in recently built or well-insulated homes.

### Stade Français - Montpellier: Yoan Tanga, the 'chameleon' returns to the forefront, a symbol of Parisian resilience.


The match between Stade Français and Montpellier highlighted the return to prominence of player Yoan Tanga, nicknamed the "chameleon" for his ability to adapt to different positions on the field. Tanga became a symbol of Parisian resilience after surviving a serious knee injury in 2018. He joined Stade Français in 2019 and quickly demonstrated his value, notably scoring several key tries in the victory against Montpellier. This performance was praised by fans and journalists, who highlighted his courage and talent. Tanga was also recognized for his social commitment, participating in charitable activities and supporting humanitarian causes. His return to prominence is a sign of the fighting spirit of Stade Français and the strength of their team.
