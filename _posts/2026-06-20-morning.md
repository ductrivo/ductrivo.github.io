### Algae and waste: the Trump administration's $14 million sewage treatment project


The Chesapeake Bay drainage basin project, funded by Donald Trump with $14 million in 2010, was a failure. The goal was to improve water quality by reducing algae and organic waste in the basin, which is one of the most polluted in the United States. However, after eight years of work, no significant improvement was observed.

The project was criticized for its lack of planning and clear objectives. Experts pointed out that the measures taken were insufficient to reduce pollution in the basin, which is affected by multiple sources of pollution, including intensive agriculture, industry, and urban runoff.

Furthermore, the project was criticized for its lack of collaboration with local and regional authorities. Environmental organizations also pointed out that the project had not taken into account the potential effects on the basin's biodiversity.

Finally, the project was criticized for its high cost. The total cost of the work was estimated at $14 million, but it is difficult to determine whether this amount was used effectively. Local authorities emphasized that the funds could have been used more effectively to reduce pollution in the basin.

In conclusion, the Chesapeake Bay drainage basin project was a failure. Critics highlighted its lack of planning and clear objectives, its lack of collaboration with local and regional authorities, as well as its high cost.
