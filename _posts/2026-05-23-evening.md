### Did you know that Picardy had a unique connection to the world? The Familistère in Guise, in the Aisne department.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/b6ef0518-8116-4892-b767-db3dbe761c3b/464x261_sc_famlistrere.jpg"
    alt="Did you know that Picardy had a unique connection to the world? The Familistère in Guise, in the Aisne department."
    caption="Saviez-vous que la Picardie abritait un lien unique au monde ? Le Familistère de Guise dans l’Aisne" %}


The Familistère of Guise, located in the Aisne department in Picardy, is unique in the world. Designed in the 19th century by industrialist Jean-Baptiste André Godin, it is a vast social palace that housed hundreds of working-class families. The aim was to improve their daily lives by creating a community environment where housing, school, daycare, and workshops were grouped together. The architecture of the Familistère is remarkable, with its buildings made of stone and its neo-Gothic style. Today, it is a listed historical monument and welcomes visitors who come to discover this unique social experience in French history.

Attal is also known for his generosity towards the citizens of Pergamon, who benefited from reduced taxes and easier access to public services. He also founded schools, libraries, and theaters in Pergamon, which contributed to the cultural prosperity of the city.

Attal died in 86 BC, leaving his kingdom to Rome as his legacy. The Roman Senate decided to make it a Roman province, which marked the end of the kingdom of Pergamon. Attal was buried in a tomb in Pergamon, which became a place of pilgrimage for Greek and Roman citizens.
