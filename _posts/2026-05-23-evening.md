### Heat: Why isn't the heatwave alert triggered when temperatures exceed 30°C in France?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/e1a4322a-3883-4e66-befb-9777c96ff2f7/140x140_sc_000-17x3d0.jpg"
    alt="Heat: Why isn't the heatwave alert triggered when temperatures exceed 30°C in France?"
    caption="Chaleur : pourquoi la vigilance canicule n'est pas déclenchée alors que les températures dépassent les 30°C en France ?" %}


The summer heatwave is about to arrive in France with temperatures expected to exceed 30°C over the Pentecost weekend, even in some parts of northern France. However, no heatwave alert has been triggered. Here are the reasons:

A heatwave alert is triggered when extreme weather conditions are forecast for at least three consecutive days, with a high risk of excessive heat for human health. To trigger the alert, temperatures must reach or exceed 35°C in certain areas of the country for at least three consecutive days.

Currently, the weather forecasts did not predict that these conditions would be reached over the Pentecost weekend. However, it is important to remain vigilant, as temperatures can increase and heatwave conditions can be triggered later in the season.

It is recommended that vulnerable people, such as the elderly or those with cardiovascular disease, avoid staying in the sun for too long and drink plenty of water to stay hydrated. It is also important to protect yourself from the sun with a hat, sunglasses and sunscreen.

Finally, it is important to note that heatwave conditions can have negative effects on human and animal health, as well as on the environment. It is therefore important to stay informed and follow the advice of the meteorological authorities to stay safe.
