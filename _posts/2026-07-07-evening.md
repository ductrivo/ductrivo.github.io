Luis Diaz had complete control over the field during the Indigenous Peoples' America Cup. This significant experience transformed his life. The Indigenous Peoples' America Cup is an international sporting competition that brings together teams from different indigenous nations to promote the culture and rights of indigenous peoples. Luis Diaz was responsible for the logistical, financial, and security aspects of the event, which required close coordination with other members of his collective as well as local authorities. This experience allowed him to develop skills in leadership, organization, and managing complex projects. Furthermore, he was able to strengthen the ties between indigenous peoples in South America and promote the visibility and recognition of their cultural and territorial rights. Today, Luis Diaz continues to work for the defense of the rights of indigenous peoples in Ecuador and to share his experience with other indigenous communities around the world.
