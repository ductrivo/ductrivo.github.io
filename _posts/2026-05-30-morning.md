### Tour de France 2026: A specially chartered Inoui high-speed train, operated by SNCF, will transport the cyclists from Grenoble to Paris before the final stage.


The 2026 Tour de France will feature a unique collaboration between SNCF and the event: a TGV Inoui will be specially chartered to transport the riders from Grenoble to Paris before the final stage. This measure is taken to improve the safety of the participants by avoiding congested road traffic. The TGV Inoui, which has a maximum speed of 320 km/h, will be equipped with a special compartment for the riders and their equipment. This initiative is an innovative example of collaboration between rail transport and professional cycling.

### Ukraine War: 3D detection, 'tin shield'... What is the ST-68 radar station, 'Tin Shield', destroyed by Kyiv in occupied Crimea?


The war in Ukraine has seen numerous technological developments, including the destruction of a radar station called ST-68, also known as the "Tin Shield." This radar station was located in the Crimean Peninsula, occupied by Kyiv.

The ST-68 was a long-range Russian military installation, capable of performing 3D detection and providing accurate information on the positions and movements of enemy forces. It was considered a key element of the Russian air defense system in the region.

The destruction of this radar station by Kyiv was seen as a strategic victory for Ukraine, as it reduced the detection and reaction capabilities of Russian forces in the region. However, Russia claimed that its destruction had no significant impact on its air defense system.

The ST-68 is one of many military installations that have been destroyed or damaged during the war in Ukraine, as part of a complex conflict between Ukrainian and Russian forces in the Crimean region.

*[Source](https://www.leparisien.fr/international/ukraine/detection-3d-bouclier-en-etain-quest-ce-que-la-station-radar-st-68-tin-shield-detruite-par-lukraine-en-crimee-occupee-30-05-2026-6B2YEDLPMRCTHDGV664IM6UNIY.php)*


### Roland Garros: French player Halys defeated, and after Djokovic's exit, Alexander Zverev is on track to win the final.


The Roland-Garros tournament has seen a new development with French player Ugo Hally winning in the semi-final against Italian Lorenzo Musetti (6-3, 6-2, 6-4). In parallel, Novak Djokovic was eliminated from the competition after his defeat against Russian Karen Khachanov in the quarter-final (7-5, 6-7, 6-1, 6-3).

In this context, Alexander Zverev is now in a position to win the final. He will face Ugo Hally in the final scheduled for Sunday. If Zverev wins, it will be his first title at the Roland-Garros tournament and his fourth major title after those won at the Australian Open in 2018, at Wimbledon in 2021, and at the US Open in 2020.

In the semi-final, Zverev defeated Serbian Filip Krajinovic (6-3, 6-4, 6-2). Hally, on the other hand, overturned the odds by eliminating the defending champion and world number one, Djokovic.

This edition of Roland-Garros is marked by the absence of some major players such as Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Daniil Medvedev, who all withdrew before the start of the tournament.

### 'There were moments when I could barely stand on my legs': Why Djokovic fell in the third round at the French Open


Novak Djokovic was eliminated in the third round of the 2021 French Open tournament. Despite being the favorite, he lost to Spanish player Alejandro Davidovich Fokina in five sets (6-7(5), 6-3, 6-4, 3-6, 6-0). Djokovic said after the match that he had physical problems during the match. He also mentioned that his preparation for the tournament was not ideal due to restrictions related to the Covid-19 pandemic, which prevented his training and competitive matches before the French Open. Djokovic also lost in the third round of the 2021 Wimbledon tournament against Croatian player Denis Kudla, ending his streak of 27 consecutive wins on clay.
