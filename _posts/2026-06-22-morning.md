### Heatwave: Why we tolerate high temperatures more or less


Heatwave: Reasons why some people tolerate high temperatures better

A heatwave is a period of time characterized by extremely high temperatures, which can have a significant impact on human health and well-being. The reasons why some individuals tolerate high temperatures better than others are multiple and complex.

One reason is related to physiological acclimatization, which occurs when the human body gradually adapts to local climatic conditions. People who live in hot climates all year round are generally better acclimatized to high temperatures than those who live in colder climates.

Another reason is related to thermal resistance, which depends on several factors such as age, sex, height, and body mass. Older people, men, and individuals with a higher body mass generally have better thermal resistance and can therefore tolerate high temperatures better.

Finally, lifestyle and cultural practices can also have an impact on a person's ability to tolerate high temperatures. For example, some cultures have developed strategies to minimize the impact of high temperatures, such as wearing light clothing and living in well-ventilated homes.

In conclusion, a person's ability to tolerate high temperatures is influenced by several factors such as physiological acclimatization, thermal resistance, lifestyle, and cultural practices. People who live in hot climates all year round are generally better acclimatized to high temperatures than those who live in colder climates.
