### Ukraine sends 60 drones to Moscow, Russia closes the four airports in the city.


On October 14, 2022, Ukraine launched a surprise attack by sending 60 drones towards Moscow, the capital of Russia. The drones targeted military and critical infrastructure within the city. In response to this attack, Russia closed the four airports in Moscow to secure the airspace around the capital. There were no reported casualties in this attack, but damage to property was reported in several locations within the city. Russian authorities stated that the attack was a violation of international law and promised a strong response if the attacks continued. Ukraine stated that it was acting in self-defense and accused Russia of violating international law by continuing to bombard civilian areas in eastern Ukraine. The two countries have been in conflict since 2014, with increasing tensions since the beginning of Russia's invasion of Ukraine in February 2022.

### 18 people missing after an explosion near a liquefied gas production site in Qatar, which was attacked in March by Iran.


On the night of April 27-28, an explosion occurred near a liquefied natural gas plant in Qatar, resulting in at least 18 deaths and several injuries. The plant is located in the Ras Laffan industrial area, about 70 kilometers northwest of Doha.

The explosion was followed by a fire, which was quickly brought under control by local emergency services. Qatari authorities stated that the incident had no connection to the ongoing conflict between Qatar and Iran, as the area is secured by military forces.

The plant belongs to RasGas, a Qatari oil and gas company that produces liquefied natural gas for export to countries such as Germany, Japan, and South Korea. The company stated that it was cooperating with authorities to investigate the incident.

Families of the missing have been informed and are awaiting further information about the cause of the explosion. Qatari authorities have promised a thorough investigation to determine the circumstances of the incident and to ensure the safety of workers in the country's oil plants.
