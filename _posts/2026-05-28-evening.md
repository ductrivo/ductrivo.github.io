### Drowning risks: what behaviors should be adopted to swim safely?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/c1b86366-6711-438c-b799-0ca58b377cbb/464x261_sc_maxnewsfrfour266331.jpg"
    alt="Drowning risks: what behaviors should be adopted to swim safely?"
    caption="Risques de noyade : quels sont les comportements à adopter pour se baigner en toute sécurité ?" %}


In late May, during the hot weather, many French people are seeking coolness by swimming in various locations. To ensure safe swimming and avoid the risk of drowning, it is important to adopt certain behaviors.

For children, it is recommended not to leave minors unattended near the water. It is also important that adults are able to swim and have floats or life jackets for young children.

Swimmers should be aware of dangerous signs, such as restricted areas, warning signs, or strong current warnings. They should also avoid swimming too far from the shore and swimming in deep water.

It is important not to drink alcohol before swimming, as it can impair judgment and coordination. Swimmers should also avoid entering fishing or boating areas.

Night swimming is particularly dangerous and should be avoided, as the risk of drowning increases due to poor visibility. Swimmers should also wear appropriate clothing for the water temperature and protect themselves from the sun.

Finally, it is important to follow the safety rules established by local authorities and to inform lifeguards in case of danger or problems. By following these tips, French people can swim safely and enjoy the coolness of the water without risk.
