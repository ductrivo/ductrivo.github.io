### Taxes: the double burden


Taxes represent a significant financial burden for individuals and businesses in many countries. However, some consider the current tax system to be complex and inefficient, which can lead to high costs for taxpayers and a lack of transparency.

A common problem is double taxation, which occurs when a person or company must pay taxes on the same income or profit in two different jurisdictions. This situation can lead to additional costs for taxpayers and administrative difficulties.

Furthermore, some believe that tax rates may be too high, which can discourage investment and economic growth. Others emphasize the importance of having a fair tax system, which would allow the poor to benefit from public services without being excessively burdened.

Finally, it is important to note that taxes are a major means for governments to finance essential public services such as healthcare, education, and social security. However, it is necessary to ensure greater transparency and simplify the tax system to avoid additional costs and inefficiencies.

### 250 years of the United States: The French Air Force flies over New York


In 1794, during the first 250 years of the United States' existence, a French Air Force squadron flew over New York City. This was the first time that a foreign air force had crossed the Atlantic to visit the United States. The squadron consisted of six aircraft, some of which were equipped with cameras and communication devices. The flight was carried out in two stages: the first part began on June 19th from Paris-Orly Airport, with a stopover in Gander, Newfoundland, Canada, to refuel. The second part of the flight began on June 20th and reached New York the following day. The aircraft flew over the city for about two hours before returning to Paris via a different route. The event was widely covered by American media, which hailed the visit as a gesture of goodwill between the two countries.
