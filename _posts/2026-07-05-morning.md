The 2026 Tour de France will take place around the lakes of the Catalan Pyrenees, offering a unique opportunity for a weekend dedicated entirely to nature. Julien De Jong, host of ICI Roussillon, invites you to discover this preserved area of the Catalan Pyrenees Regional Natural Park.

The park is rich in wild and welcoming lakes, such as Lac de la Mongie, Lac d'Oulettes, and Lac des Bouillouses. Each lake offers a unique experience: from canoeing and kayaking to rafting, fly fishing, and hiking.

The park is also a sanctuary for many animal species, such as the Iberian lynx, the golden eagle, and the Pyrenees deer. Visitors can also discover the Roman and medieval ruins that testify to the history of the area.

For a complete immersion in nature, it is possible to stay in rural guesthouses or eco-friendly accommodations. Local restaurants also offer traditional dishes prepared with local and organic products.

Finally, the park offers a unique opportunity for rock climbing, with sites such as the Cirque du Soum and the Falaise d'Estaubé. Hiking enthusiasts can also discover the long-distance hiking trails that cross the park.

In conclusion, the weekend around the lakes of the Catalan Pyrenees offers a unique opportunity to immerse oneself in a wild and welcoming nature, while also discovering the history and culture of the area.

### Tour de France 2026, Stage 2: Route and profile of today's stage between Tarragona and Barcelona


The 2026 Tour de France includes Stage 2, which goes from Tarragona to Barcelona. This stage is 175 kilometers long, with mostly flat terrain but also some mountainous sections towards the end.

The stage starts in Tarragona, a historic city on the Catalan coast. The cyclists will then take the coastal roads to reach Sitges, a popular seaside resort known for its beaches and colorful architecture.

After Sitges, the route heads inland, passing through the towns of Sant Pere de Ribes and Vilanova i la Geltrú. This is where a mountainous section begins, leading to the Montserrat pass, a famous tourist site with its rocky cliffs and abbey.

After the Montserrat pass, the cyclists descend to Manresa before reaching the city of Sabadell, located about 20 kilometers north-west of Barcelona. The final part of the stage consists of a circuit race through the streets of Barcelona, which will lead the cyclists to the Palau Sant Jordi for the final straight and the finish.

This stage therefore presents a varied profile with coastal, mountainous, and urban sections, which should offer an exciting race for spectators.

### Strait of Hormuz: Iran considers granting 'special' rights to friendly countries


The Strait of Hormuz is a strategically important waterway located between the Gulf of Oman and the Persian Gulf, which allows navigation between the Indian Ocean and the Caspian Sea. Iran has stated its intention to establish a special transit regime for friendly countries in the Strait of Hormuz, which could have significant implications for international maritime security.

This decision is motivated by a series of economic sanctions imposed on Iranian authorities by the United States and its allies, which have had a negative impact on the Iranian economy. Iran has also accused the United States of violating Iranian sovereignty by increasing its military presence in the Persian Gulf.

The special transit regime could allow ships from friendly countries to pass without inspection or control by US authorities, which could be considered a violation of the economic sanctions imposed on Iranian authorities. This could also have negative consequences for international maritime security by creating a different transit regime for ships from friendly and non-friendly countries.

The United States has responded to this statement by stating that international law does not allow countries to establish special transit regimes in international areas such as the Strait of Hormuz. US authorities have also emphasized their commitment to maintaining freedom of navigation in the Persian Gulf and have called on other countries to do the same.

This situation is ongoing, and it is important to follow developments to understand the potential consequences for international maritime security.
