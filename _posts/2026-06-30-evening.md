### After the heatwave, how long will it take for your home to cool down?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/aa36045a-6922-49ab-9ffc-9e3fdf6adbbd/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="After the heatwave, how long will it take for your home to cool down?"
    caption="Après la canicule, en combien de temps votre logement va-t-il refroidir ?" %}


After a heatwave, your home will require several days to cool down completely. Buildings accumulate heat during heatwaves, which slows down the cooling process. The time it takes to cool your home depends on several factors such as the size of the building, the thermal insulation, and natural or artificial ventilation.

To speed up the cooling process, it is recommended to open windows and doors to allow for better ventilation. Using an extractor fan can also help to remove the accumulated heat from your home. In addition, using reflective curtains or shutters can also help to cool your home by blocking sunlight.

Finally, it is important to note that the time it takes to cool your home can vary depending on the outside temperature and the effectiveness of the building's thermal insulation. Therefore, it is recommended to wait several days before considering that your home is completely cooled.

### 'Harmful consequences for users': why the plastic bottle deposit scheme is not working


The bill aimed at banning plastic bottles in commercial establishments was not adopted by the French Parliament, despite popular support and environmental concerns related to plastic waste. Opponents of the bill highlighted technical and economic difficulties for businesses, as well as uncertainties regarding the management of waste resulting from the ban. Some also criticized the lack of a comprehensive solution for reducing plastic waste in the environment. Supporters of the bill emphasized that the ban on plastic bottles would be an important step towards more sustainable waste management and reducing environmental impacts. They also pointed to the harmful effects on human and animal health associated with the use of plastic bottles, as well as the economic cost of plastic waste in the environment. Opponents of the bill also pointed out that the consequences for users could be harmful, particularly for low-income individuals who often use plastic bottles to drink tap water. They also pointed to the lack of practical and affordable alternatives for these people. Supporters of the bill emphasized that existing alternatives, such as glass or cardboard bottles, are more sustainable and less polluting, and that they can be made more accessible through government support measures.

### Financial fair play: UEFA fines Strasbourg and Nice several million euros


Financial Fair Play is a measure taken by the Union of European Football Associations (UEFA) to encourage clubs to manage their finances responsibly. In this context, the French clubs RC Strasbourg Alsace and OGC Nice were sanctioned by UEFA for financial irregularities.

RC Strasbourg was fined 10 million euros due to its excessive debt and lack of financial transparency. OGC Nice, on the other hand, was fined 25 million euros for exceeding the limits imposed by the Financial Fair Play rule regarding debt and losses.

The sanctions were decided after an investigation by UEFA into the finances of the clubs involved, which revealed irregularities such as undeclared player transfers or unjustified loans. The clubs were also fined for each violation of the Financial Fair Play rule.

The sanctions have a significant impact on the finances of the clubs involved, who must now find ways to reduce their debt and comply with the limits imposed by UEFA. The clubs may also be excluded from European competitions if they do not comply with the rules of Financial Fair Play.

This sanction is a measure taken to encourage clubs to manage their finances responsibly and to avoid excessive debt, which can have a negative impact on their sporting and economic activities.
