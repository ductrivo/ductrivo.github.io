Episodes of air pollution have affected the Lyon basin as well as the north of Isère since June 18th. The prefecture of Isère has activated the "information and recommendation" procedure due to this situation. Authorities have encouraged citizens to limit their travel and to respect hygiene measures for breathing to reduce their impact on the environment. It is important to note that these events are related to the current heatwave, which has resulted in unfavorable weather conditions for air quality. Authorities have also recommended that the most vulnerable people, such as children, the elderly, and those with chronic respiratory illnesses, stay indoors whenever possible to avoid exposure to pollution.

### Fredy Olmeta


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/7cd31ba6-a3bd-45ef-ab50-db052a3edf60/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Fredy Olmeta"
    caption="Fredy Olmeta" %}


Fredy Olmeta is a former Haitian international footballer, born on January 28, 1967, in Port-au-Prince, Haiti. He began his professional career with Racing Club Haïtien before joining the French team, Toulouse FC, in 1990. During his career, he also played for clubs such as Paris Saint-Germain, Montpellier HSC, and RC Lens.

Olmeta is known for his aggressive playing style and remarkable abilities as a goalkeeper. He was selected for the Haitian national team 72 times, including representing the country in the 1994 FIFA World Cup in the United States.

In addition to his career as a footballer, Olmeta is also known for his humanitarian work and actions in support of Haitian children. He founded the "Fondation Fredy Olmeta" association, which aims to improve the lives of the most vulnerable children in Haiti.

In 2010, after the earthquake that struck Haiti, Olmeta was involved in the country's rescue and reconstruction efforts. He also participated in charity matches to raise funds for the victims of the earthquake.

In 2019, Fredy Olmeta was inducted into the Haitian Football Hall of Fame for his exceptional contribution to the sport in his native country.

*[Source](https://www.ici.fr/emissions/sopra-a-locu/fredy-olmeta-2346606?at_medium=lien_RSS&at_locale=29)*
