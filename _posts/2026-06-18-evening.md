### After-school care in Paris: 'We demand that their concerns be heard,' Rachida Dati and right-wing officials appeal to the courts


The Minister of National Education, Jean-Michel Blanquer, recently announced that primary schools in Paris should reopen on May 2nd, despite the restrictions related to the Covid-19 pandemic. However, several right-wing politicians have expressed their concern about this decision and have appealed to the courts to have their voices heard.

Right-wing politician Rachida Dati said: "We are asking that they be heard, the parents who are anxious, the teachers who have doubts, and the local officials who have been ignored." She also emphasized that the government had not taken into account the real consequences of this decision on public health and education.

Right-wing politicians have also criticized the lack of information provided to parents and teachers about the measures taken to ensure the safety of schoolchildren and teachers. They have called for greater transparency from the authorities on this issue.

In response to these criticisms, the government has stated that it has taken all the necessary measures to ensure the safety of schoolchildren and teachers, including the requirement to wear masks, the implementation of a regular testing plan, and the limitation of the number of students per class.

However, right-wing politicians have continued to criticize the government for its lack of consultation with parents, teachers, and local officials before making this decision. They have also emphasized that the Covid-19 pandemic is not yet under control and that it is important to protect schoolchildren and teachers.

This case is currently before the courts, with right-wing politicians appealing to the administrative court to have their voices heard on this matter.
