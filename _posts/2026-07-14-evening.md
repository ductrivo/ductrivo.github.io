### 'She starts licking again, I'm reassured': the incredible mobilization to save 80 dogs from the Fontainebleau fire


On May 15th, a fire broke out in the Fontainebleau forest, threatening the lives of 80 dogs that were being kept in conditional freedom. An extraordinary mobilization was launched to save these animals. Firefighters, forest rangers, and volunteers joined forces to combat the fire and rescue the dogs. Rescue teams also arrived from Paris and Marne-la-Vallée.

After several hours of fighting, the firefighters managed to control the fire. The dogs were then treated by veterinarians and volunteers who transported them to temporary shelters. Some had suffered severe burns, but most were saved thanks to the rapid and coordinated action of the rescuers.

This tragedy has caused a great deal of emotion in the country, with thousands of messages of support on social media for the rescued dogs and the dedicated rescuers. The authorities have also announced that they will conduct an investigation to determine the cause of the fire.

### 'You won't face anything at all, you'll go home!' In Brittany, Jean-Luc Mélenchon chases Édouard Philippe.


In Brittany, the candidate from La France Insoumise, Jean-Luc Mélenchon, engaged in a political confrontation with Prime Minister Édouard Philippe during a demonstration against the pension reform law. Mélenchon told Philippe: "You won't face anything, you'll go home," meaning that the Prime Minister should give up his position due to the strong opposition to the law. This political confrontation takes place in the context of a nationwide protest against the pension reform, which includes, among other things, an increase in the retirement age and a reduction in retirement benefits for the least qualified workers. The demonstrations were marked by violent incidents, with hundreds of arrests and injuries.
