### The National Assembly approves the 'exceptional' use of genetic genealogy to help solve investigations.


The French National Assembly has granted, on an exceptional basis, permission for the use of genetic genealogy in investigations. This measure will provide additional evidence to resolve certain difficult cases. This is a temporary decision, taken in response to a request from the French government. Genetic genealogy involves analyzing DNA to establish family relationships between individuals. This technique can be useful in criminal or judicial investigations, particularly for identifying suspects or victims. However, this decision is controversial and raises concerns about the protection of citizens' privacy. Measures will be taken to ensure that the use of genetic genealogy respects fundamental rights and data protection laws.
