### Will bottled water really be banned this summer in the European Union, as claimed by an RN MEP?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/5199ea62-95bc-48b8-b858-8a5c52a72c7b/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Will bottled water really be banned this summer in the European Union, as claimed by an RN MEP?"
    caption="Les packs d'eau vont-ils vraiment être interdits cet été dans l'Union européenne, comme l'affirme une eurodéputée RN ?" %}


Plastic bottles will not be banned in the European Union this year, contrary to what the RN MEP Virginie Joron claims on her social media. In fact, a European regulation on plastic packaging does not provide for a ban on the sale of plastic bottles from August 12, 2026. However, this regulation includes an exemption for certain specific films used for these products.

Thus, the Massif Central has acquired an important place in the route of the Tour de France, and its presence has become recurring since 2022.

### World Cup: 'In the best interest of Senegalese football,' coach Pape Thiaw fired


The Senegalese national football coach, Pape Thiaw, was sacked on Thursday, October 28th. According to the Senegalese Football Federation (FSF), this decision was made "in the best interests of Senegalese football." The exact reason has not been revealed, but the national team's results have been mixed since Thiaw took over in 2015. It should be noted that Senegal finished third in the 2019 Africa Cup of Nations, but failed to qualify for the 2018 World Cup and were eliminated in the first round of the 2021 Africa Cup of Nations. The FSF announced that a new coach would be appointed soon.

### Salary, rest, small perks… In Pierre Latour's new life, the cyclist who has become a VIP chauffeur for the Tour de France.


Pierre Latour, a former high-level climber, now has a new job: he has become a VIP chauffeur for the Tour de France. His salary and working conditions are different from those he knew as a professional cyclist. He no longer reaches the summits of the mountains, but he has the opportunity to attend every stage of the Tour de France from the cockpit of his car. His job is to transport VIPs and sponsors to the starting and finishing locations of the stages. He works for a company specializing in the organization of major sporting events.
