    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/60544508-ca17-4570-884d-e6eaf22a113e/920x517?webp=false"

    caption="CARTE - Sécheresse : quel niveau d'alerte et quelles restrictions d'eau près de chez vous ?" %}


Drought Map: Local Alert Level and Water Restrictions

In France, several regions are currently experiencing water restrictions due to drought. To find out the alert level and restrictions in force near you, see our interactive map. It allows you to graphically visualize the extent of the water crisis and the measures taken in each region.

The alert levels are defined by the French Ministry of Ecology:
- level 1: vigilance, warning to consumers;
- level 2: restriction of non-essential uses;
- level 3: partial or total prohibition of non-essential uses.

Water restrictions can take different forms depending on the region and alert level:
- limiting the number of car washes;
- prohibiting the washing of sidewalks;
- restricting the irrigation of gardens;
- prohibiting washing cars at home;
- prohibiting washing cars at gas stations.

It is important to respect these restrictions in order to limit water consumption and help preserve water resources.

### The signature dish of a chef: the sea urchin, Benjamin Mathieu's secret weapon


The signature product of chef Benjamin Mathieu is poutargue, a closely guarded recipe that has been passed down for generations. This traditional recipe is used to prepare a typical sauce from the Brittany region of France. Poutargue is made from dried and salted cod, which is then ground into a fine, smooth paste. This preparation is then stored in an earthenware pot to ensure its shelf life. The case was covered by international media and highlighted the importance of customs in protecting cultural property and stopping the illegal trafficking of artworks. The suspects were arrested and the paintings were returned to their rightful owners.
