### Grand Start in Barcelona, favorites, key stages: everything you need to know about the 2026 Tour de France


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/f94ca4ed-f228-4b59-92f5-d78c2b69287f/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Grand Start in Barcelona, favorites, key stages: everything you need to know about the 2026 Tour de France"
    caption="Grand Départ à Barcelone, favoris, étapes clés : tout savoir sur le Tour de France 2026" %}


The 2026 Tour de France starts on Saturday, July 4th in Barcelona, marking the 113th edition of this cycling competition. The event includes 21 stages, 184 riders, 23 teams, and was initially planned for 2023 but has been postponed to the following year.

The favorites for this edition include cycling champions such as Tadej Pogačar, the winner of the 2021 and 2022 Tour de France, as well as Geraint Thomas, the winner in 2018. In addition, Jonas Vingegaard, who finished second in the 2022 Tour de France, is also considered a favorite for the final victory.

Key stages of this edition include a double ascent of the Col d'Huez before the arrival in Paris on July 26th. Other notable stages include climbs to the cols of Peyresourde, Tourmalet, and Aubisque in the Pyrenees, as well as the individual time trial in Bordeaux.

The route of this edition of the Tour de France is 3,408 kilometers long and covers all of France, starting in Barcelona and ending in Paris. The host cities for the stages also include Toulouse, Montpellier, Nîmes, Avignon, Grenoble, Lyon, Dijon, Auxerre, Reims, Amiens, Rouen, Caen, Cherbourg, and Bordeaux.

This edition of the Tour de France is eagerly awaited by cycling fans around the world, offering a spectacular and demanding competition for the riders.

### The regional audit office points to 'poor management' of the Monteynard lake.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/4bb3d688-62e7-4e13-8bfa-05cd9269fe8c/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="The regional audit office points to 'poor management' of the Monteynard lake."
    caption="La Chambre régionale des comptes pointe une 'gestion défaillante' du lac de Monteynard" %}


The report by the Regional Chamber of Accounts reveals an inefficient management of the Monteynard lake, the third tourist site in Isère, studied between 2019 and 2025. The Chamber found problems in the financial, technical, and administrative management of the lake, which it described as deficient. The authorities were criticized for their lack of efficiency and transparency, as well as for their inability to implement the necessary measures to improve the management of the site. The Chamber also highlighted the absence of a global strategy for the sustainable development of the lake and insufficient measures to protect the environment. The recommendations include better planning, better financial and technical management, as well as greater transparency and strengthened governance.

Episode 5: In a Vintage Rock 'n' Roll (1976). The episode deals with the musical structure of the Carpenters and the detailed creation of a song. It offers insights into the creative process and the encounters that shaped this work. The authors explore how Richard Carpenter used classical composition techniques to create pop melodies, as well as the influence of rock 'n' roll on their music. Finally, they address the commercial success of the Carpenters and their impact on popular music in the 1970s.

### Handball: 'I accepted a role with significant responsibilities,' Didier Dinart becomes the new coach of Luxembourg.


The French handball player, Didier Dinart, has accepted a high-level position by becoming the new coach of the Luxembourg national team. He succeeds Guy Mercier, who had held the position since 2014.

Dinart is known for his experience and expertise in the world of handball. He has coached several French teams, including US Ivry Handball, Montpellier Handball, and Paris Saint-Germain Handball. With these teams, he has won numerous national and international titles.

As the selector for Luxembourg, Dinart will take charge of both the men's and women's national teams. He succeeds Guy Mercier, who led the men's team to its highest ranking in its history (24th place).

Dinart stated that he was happy to have accepted this role and to be able to contribute to the development of Luxembourg handball. He also emphasized that his priority would be to work with young Luxembourg players to give them the opportunity to progress in the sport.

This appointment marks a significant turning point for Luxembourg handball, which hopes to continue to progress under Dinart's leadership.

### The House of Horror: 16 children trapped in a 13m2 room... for 4 years


The place in question is known as the House of Horror, located in Cleveland, Ohio, in the United States. From 2003 to 2007, sixteen children were held in a room of 13 square meters, about four times smaller than a standard bathroom. The conditions were extremely detrimental to their health and physical and mental development.

The children were subjected to physical, emotional, and sexual abuse, as well as inadequate nutrition. They had no access to fresh air, daylight, or a place to rest. The conditions were so bad that several children developed serious mental and physical health problems, including eating disorders, hygiene problems, and physical injuries.

The children were released in 2007 after an investigation by social services and the police. The biological parents of some of the children were accused of neglect and abuse, while others were placed in foster families or institutions.

The House of Horror sparked widespread public outrage and led to significant reforms in the child protection system in the United States. Authorities have implemented measures to prevent such abuse from happening again, including stricter controls on foster families and training programs for social workers.
