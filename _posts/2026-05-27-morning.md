### Long before the Roman numerals, how did the Gauls count?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/b5560375-23fb-46db-9e66-17537f207361/464x261_sc_gettyimages-2198585722.jpg"
    alt="Long before the Roman numerals, how did the Gauls count?"
    caption="Bien avant les chiffres romains, comment comptaient les Gaulois ?" %}


Before the appearance of Roman numerals, the Gauls used a counting method based on a wooden stick and tokens. This method was already effective among merchants before the arrival of the Romans in Gaul. The wooden stick was used to mark units, while the tokens were used for tens, hundreds, and thousands. This method was used for several centuries before being replaced by Roman writing.

*[Source](https://www.ici.fr/emissions/stephane-bern-voyage-dans-le-temps/bien-avant-les-chiffres-romains-comment-comptaient-les-gaulois-6361973?at_medium=lien_RSS&at_locale=29)*

This text describes the publication of a book titled "It's Simple and It's Beautiful", which recounts five years of research on the rural houses of the Cap Sizun in Brittany. The book was published on May 15th and highlights the cultural heritage and specific architecture of Cap Sizun in order to better preserve them. It offers a detailed account of the construction, evolution, and use of rural houses in this region, as well as information on their history and cultural significance. The book is intended for those interested in traditional Breton architecture and who want to learn more about the cultural heritage of Cap Sizun.

The Drôme department is currently facing a difficult situation on construction sites in Valence due to the extreme heat. Workers are at risk of suffocation, which has forced some of them to adjust their working hours to avoid the hottest parts of the day. This means that some are working earlier in the morning or later in the afternoon to avoid the unfavorable weather conditions. Workers have no choice, as the construction sites are in progress and they must continue to work to complete their projects.
