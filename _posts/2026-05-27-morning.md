### 'There's an emergency!' Norman cheeses fight back in the face of crisis


In a critical situation, the cheeses of Normandy launch a counter-attack. The traditional dairy products of this French region are threatened by a decline in their popularity and an increase in imports of foreign cheeses. To respond to this crisis, the producers of Normandy have implemented several measures to promote their cheeses and strengthen their image of quality.

First, they created a quality label to certify that the cheeses are produced using traditional methods and in the region of Normandy. Then, they launched a advertising campaign to promote their products to French and foreign consumers. They also organized events and exhibitions to allow consumers to discover the cheeses of Normandy and meet the local producers.

The producers of Normandy have also worked with the French government to implement measures to protect traditional dairy products. They have requested financial assistance to support producers and help develop foreign markets. Finally, they have collaborated with other agricultural organizations to defend the interests of the producers of Normandy cheeses.

Despite these measures, the situation remains concerning for the producers of Normandy. They continue to face strong competition and economic difficulties. However, they hope that their efforts to promote their product and strengthen their image of quality will allow them to maintain and increase their popularity among French and foreign consumers.

### 'The symbol of an agriculture dying in our villages': Cahagnes, in Calvados, says goodbye to its dairy farm


The village of Cahagnes, located in the Calvados department, has seen its dairy farm close, a symbol of declining agriculture in rural France. The closure of this agricultural business, which employed around twenty people, is due to economic reasons. The cost of production has increased and the competition has been too strong for the company to remain profitable. This closure is a major blow for local farmers, who are seeing their business decline. It also risks having consequences on the rural landscape, with a loss of diversity and an increase in depopulation in rural areas.
