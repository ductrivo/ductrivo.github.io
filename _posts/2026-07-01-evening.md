### What changes does the new maternity leave, which comes into effect on Wednesday, July 1st, bring?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/2c4a9f14-eabb-4427-b324-5a13f4baeffb/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="What changes does the new maternity leave, which comes into effect on Wednesday, July 1st, bring?"
    caption="Ce que change le nouveau congé de naissance, qui entre en vigueur mercredi 1er juillet" %}


The new parental leave, which comes into effect on July 1, 2026, now offers a leave period of one to two months for each parent, depending on their choice. This additional leave is in addition to existing maternity and paternity leave. Here are the key changes for parents:

1. Length of leave: Parents can now choose between a one-month or two-month parental leave, in addition to current maternity and paternity leave.
2. Eligibility: All parents, biological or adoptive, are eligible for this new leave.
3. Payment: The parent's salary during leave will be replaced by paid leave benefits paid by the state. Details on the amount and duration of these benefits will be specified later.
4. Objective: The main objective of this new leave is to help parents spend more time with their child at the beginning of its life, while supporting the balance between work and family life.

### Tickets are now available to visit the Perret Tower in Grenoble.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/af7148a9-5489-431a-8e4c-a1baf0394a4c/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Tickets are now available to visit the Perret Tower in Grenoble."
    caption="La billetterie pour visiter la tour Perret à Grenoble est ouverte" %}


The Perret Tower in Grenoble is opening its ticket sales for public visits. After a closure of 60 years, the Perret Tower will reopen its doors on Saturday, July 11th. This iconic example of French modern architecture, built between 1922 and 1932 by Auguste Perret, stands 45 meters tall and offers panoramic views of the city of Grenoble. Visits will be organized in groups, with guides to explain the history and architecture of the tower. Tickets can be purchased online or on site.
