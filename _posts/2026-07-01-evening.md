### Stéphane Plaza, re-examined in appeal for domestic violence: 'I don't care about being back on TV'


Stéphane Plaza, a former French footballer, has been re-tried in an appeals court for charges of domestic violence. He stated: "I have no interest in returning to television."

Plaza was convicted in the first instance in 2019 for physically assaulting his wife, who accused him of repeated domestic abuse. He was sentenced to a suspended sentence and a fine of 3,000 euros.

The appeals court heard arguments from both sides on January 28, 2021, to determine whether Plaza should be convicted in the appeal or not. The details of the court's decision have not yet been made public.

Plaza played for several French clubs, including AS Monaco and Paris Saint-Germain, before retiring from football in 2013. He also participated in the 2006 FIFA World Cup with the French team.

Since his departure from football, Plaza has worked as a commentator for French television channels, including RMC Sport and Canal+. He has also participated in reality shows such as "Dancing with the Stars" and "Everyone Wants Their Turn".

The appeals court heard the complaint of his ex-wife, who accuses him of repeated domestic abuse. The details of the court's decision have not yet been made public, but Plaza stated that he had no interest in returning to television.
