### PHOTOS: SNCF's dramatic project to reinforce the tracks at the Bourgoin-Jallieu station


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/dcc32c61-41fd-40a3-a3df-b5cddc6bbb7d/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="PHOTOS: SNCF's dramatic project to reinforce the tracks at the Bourgoin-Jallieu station"
    caption="PHOTOS : le chantier coup de poing de la SNCF pour consolider les voies de la gare de Bourgoin-Jallieu" %}


The SNCF's "major" project to reinforce the tracks at the Bourgoin-Jallieu station, located in North Isère, has been launched. This operation is necessary because tracks A and B were affected by regular water infiltration, which threatened their stability. The SNCF has taken the initiative to reinforce the platform that supports these tracks to ensure their safety and maintain the smooth operation of rail traffic. The work includes, among other things, the replacement of damaged structural elements, the repair of water infiltration, and the improvement of drainage. The SNCF has also taken into account environmental requirements to minimize negative impacts on the environment. The work is underway and is expected to be completed in the near future.

### A judge overturns Trump's immunity agreement with the US tax authorities.


Judge Naomi Reice Buchwald of the Southern District of New York has overturned the immunity agreement granted to Donald Trump by the U.S. government in 2017. The agreement had been reached to prevent lawsuits against Trump and his children in connection with the Trump family's real estate activities. The judge ruled that the agreement was unconstitutional, as it violated the separation of powers principle enshrined in the U.S. Constitution. She also found that the agreement had been reached without following the legal and constitutional process. This decision could have significant consequences for Trump, as it opens the door to new lawsuits against him and his family in connection with the Trump family's real estate activities.
