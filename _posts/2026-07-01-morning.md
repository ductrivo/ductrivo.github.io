### Mexico-Ecuador: Why was the start of the match delayed by an hour?


The match between Mexico and Ecuador, originally scheduled for October 10, 2021 as part of the 2022 FIFA World Cup qualifying round, was delayed by an hour due to a technical problem. Specifically, an extra time was needed so that players could use their digital equipment to comply with FIFA's rules regarding playing with or without the ball. This measure was taken in response to the Covid-19 pandemic, which had led to restrictions on contact between players and digital equipment. The match eventually began at 8:30 pm local time (9:30 pm in France).

### Venezuela: More than 100 migrants, expelled by the United States on the day of the twin earthquake, have gone missing.


Venezuela: More than 100 migrants have been declared missing after being expelled by the United States on the day of the double earthquake that struck the border region between the two countries. Venezuelan authorities have reported that these people had attempted to illegally cross the border to return to their country of origin after being expelled by the United States. The earthquake occurred on February 7, killing at least 43 people and injuring more than 200 in both countries. Venezuelan authorities have called for an international investigation to determine the cause of the disappearances and to ensure the protection of migrants. The United States has refused to participate in the investigation, stating that the migrants were expelled in accordance with immigration laws.
