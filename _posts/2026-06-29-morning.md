### How Obut transformed pétanque into a family empire in 70 years


The game of pétanque has undergone a spectacular transformation over the past 70 years, thanks to the French company Obut, which has specialized in the manufacture of equipment for this sport. Founded in 1952 by Jean-Louis Obut, the young entrepreneur began by producing pétanque balls from local materials such as clay and marble.

In the 1960s, demand for pétanque equipment exploded, allowing Obut to expand its product range to include more durable and weather-resistant plastic balls. Over the following decades, the company continued to innovate and improve its products, using materials such as stainless steel and polycarbonate to create high-quality pétanque balls.

In addition to its commercial success, Obut has also contributed to the popularization of pétanque in France and around the world. The company has supported pétanque events throughout the country, including national and international championships, and has created professional teams to promote the game.

Today, Obut is considered one of the leading pétanque companies in the world, with a wide range of products that include not only pétanque balls, but also balls, accessories, and equipment for pitches. The company continues to innovate and develop, using the latest technology to create high-quality products that meet the needs of pétanque players of all levels.

### 'Criminal justice is dead': lawyers and judges mobilize against lack of resources and the reform of the criminal justice system.


The French legal professionals' movement has mobilized to protest against the lack of resources and the proposed changes in the criminal justice reform. Lawyers and judges have highlighted their growing concern over the increasing number of cases, the lack of financial and human resources, and the increasing complexity of legal cases.

Legal professionals have also expressed their concerns about the government's proposed reforms, which include an increase in the number of hearings and a strengthening of electronic surveillance of defendants. They have emphasized that these changes could have a negative impact on the quality of criminal justice in France, particularly in terms of respecting the rights of defendants and the effectiveness of the judicial system.

Lawyers and judges have also called for better organization of the criminal justice system to reduce delays in trials, improve communication between the various actors in the judicial system, and strengthen international cooperation. They have also requested additional financial support to enable courts to function effectively and ensure a high-quality criminal justice system.

Finally, legal professionals have called for greater transparency in the criminal justice reform process to allow citizens to understand the proposed changes and express their opinions on them. They have also emphasized the importance of taking into account the opinions of legal professionals in the reform process in order to ensure an effective and fair criminal justice system for all.
