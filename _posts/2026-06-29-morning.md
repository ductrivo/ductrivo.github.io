### Parachuting accident: what we know about the fatal crash of a plane near Nancy


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/cc20ad4d-3113-470f-882c-8c1ebede84b0/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Parachuting accident: what we know about the fatal crash of a plane near Nancy"
    caption="Baptême de parachutisme : ce que l’on sait du crash mortel d’un avion près de Nancy" %}


On May 15th, a fatal aviation accident occurred near Nancy in the municipality of Tomblaine (Meurthe-et-Moselle). A civilian aircraft crashed on Sunday morning, killing eleven people. This accident is considered the deadliest in France for civilian aviation, excluding military and commercial transport, by the Bureau d'enquêtes et d'analyse pour la sécurité de l'aviation civile (BEA). Here are the details:

The aircraft was a Cessna 208 Caravan, used for parachute jumps. It was carrying 14 people, including 13 passengers and a pilot. The aircraft lost contact with air traffic control at approximately 3:30 PM. Emergency services were alerted and found the wreckage of the aircraft in a wooded area.

Investigators are still working to determine the exact causes of the accident, but it is possible that weather conditions played a role. The investigations are being conducted by the BEA and the National Gendarmerie.

This tragic accident has caused great emotion in the region and has been widely covered by national media. Authorities have offered their condolences to the families of the victims.
