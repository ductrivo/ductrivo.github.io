Bernadette Chirac, the wife of President Jacques Chirac and First Lady of France from 1995 to 2007, died on Saturday, June 6th, at the age of 93. She held the position of President of the Fondation Hôpitaux de Paris-Hôpitaux de France from 1994 to 2019. During her tenure, she often traveled throughout the French departments for official visits or in connection with Operation Yellow Cards.

Images show Bernadette Chirac accompanied by David Douillet or Christian Karembeu during her visits to our departments. She was present at events such as the opening ceremony of the 2004 Summer Olympic Games in Athens, where she was accompanied by judoka David Douillet, or at international football matches, such as the match between the French team and Brazil in 1998, where she was accompanied by Christian Karembeu.

In addition to her official visits, Bernadette Chirac also participated in charitable and cultural events in the French departments. She thus supported the fight against AIDS by participating in events such as the Marche pour la vie (March for Life) in 2004.

Bernadette Chirac was also active in the art world, particularly in supporting French artists and participating in contemporary art exhibitions. She thus opened the Fondation Bernadette Chirac for Contemporary Art in 2017.

Bernadette Chirac died on Saturday, June 6th, at the age of 93, leaving behind a significant career and a cultural and humanitarian legacy.

### 'They are killing our Vosges': Anger in Gérardmer over 'four-season' tourism


The title "They are destroying our Vosges" summarizes the outrage of the residents of Gérardmer, a town in the Vosges region of France, towards the development of "four-season tourism". The citizens complain that this type of tourism is devastating for their natural region and threatens the local quality of life. They have organized a protest against real estate projects that, in their view, contribute to this environmental destruction. Opponents of "four-season tourism" argue that this type of development is incompatible with the preservation of local nature and culture. They are calling for a review of tourism development policies to protect the Vosges region.

### Carpentras: New RN-led town hall cuts funding to the family planning service.


Carpentras: The new RN-led town hall puts the Family Planning program at risk by cutting its financial resources. The decision was made during the January town council meeting, where the budget proposal was approved with an absolute majority of votes. The Family Planning program is a national French program that provides financial assistance to families to help fund the education and healthcare costs of children. The new RN-led town hall has decided to eliminate funding for the program in the municipal budget, which puts the future of the Family Planning program in Carpentras at risk. Opponents of the project have criticized the decision, arguing that it could have a negative impact on the most vulnerable families in the city. They have also emphasized that the program helps to reduce poverty and social exclusion by providing resources to families in need. Supporters of the project have called for a review of the budget to include the funds necessary to maintain the Family Planning program in Carpentras.

### Bernadette Chirac had transformed her fight for her daughter Laurence into a public commitment.


Bernadette Chirac, the wife of the former president Jacques Chirac, turned her personal struggle for her daughter Laurence, who was suffering from breast cancer, into a public commitment. She pledged to raise awareness about cancer among the French population and to support victims of the disease. In 2015, she created the association "La Fondation contre le Cancer" (The Cancer Foundation), which aims to fund medical research and to provide psychological support to cancer patients. She has also written a book titled "Cancer, a love story", in which she recounts her personal experience with her daughter's illness and her own struggles to cope with her loss in 2013. Bernadette Chirac continues to advocate for cancer research and for improving the lives of patients suffering from the disease.
