### Provence combats the scourge of tropical diseases this summer: 'The tiger mosquito will no longer be a problem'


Southern France, particularly the Provence region, is currently facing an increase in tropical diseases transmitted by mosquitoes, particularly malaria and West Nile fever. This situation is due to a warmer and more humid climate, which favors the proliferation of these insects.

To combat this threat, health authorities in France are implementing preventative measures. These measures include treating wetlands to eliminate mosquitoes and their eggs, distributing personal protective products to tourists and local residents, and educating the public on how to protect themselves from mosquito bites.

Authorities have also announced that, starting this year, the tiger mosquito will no longer be allowed to enter France. This mosquito is responsible for transmitting malaria and several other tropical diseases. The measures being taken to prevent its introduction include strict border controls, the elimination of wetlands that could harbor these insects, and continuous monitoring of their presence in the country.

French health authorities have also called for international collaboration to combat mosquito-borne tropical diseases, particularly in areas where they are endemic. This collaboration includes the search for new prevention and treatment methods, as well as efforts to improve the living conditions of local populations in order to reduce their vulnerability to these diseases.

In conclusion, the Provence region is currently facing an increase in mosquito-borne tropical diseases, but French health authorities are taking measures to combat this threat and prevent the introduction of the tiger mosquito into France.
