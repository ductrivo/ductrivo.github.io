### Headaches, cramps, nausea: how to recognize the symptoms of heatstroke, which can sometimes be fatal?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/9846744c-1fec-4adb-8e00-84c1e45617d7/140x140_sc_canicule.jpg"
    alt="Headaches, cramps, nausea: how to recognize the symptoms of heatstroke, which can sometimes be fatal?"
    caption="Maux de tête, crampes, nausées : comment reconnaître les symptômes d'un coup de chaud, parfois mortel ?" %}


Heatstroke is a medical condition caused by prolonged exposure to high temperatures, which can be fatal. In France, much of the country experienced temperatures above 30°C on Sunday, weakening the bodies. One person died and eight were urgently transported in the Paris region during a run. Here's how to recognize the symptoms of heatstroke:

- Headache
- Muscle cramps
- Nausea
- Excessive fatigue
- Abdominal pain
- Confusion
- Weakness or loss of consciousness

If you or someone else experiences these symptoms, it is important to consult a doctor immediately. It is also important to stay hydrated by drinking regularly and avoiding prolonged exposure to sunlight. In extreme heat, it is recommended to protect yourself from UV rays by wearing a hat, sunglasses and a scarf or scarf to protect the skin on the neck.

### Diversity for surviving climate change and cultural cooling with David Grosclaude


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/30d2a45b-6f0a-4cbd-b64d-4303a483fda4/464x261_sc_35855.jpg"
    alt="Diversity for surviving climate change and cultural cooling with David Grosclaude"
    caption="La diversité pour survivre au réchauffement climatique et au refroidissement culturel avec David Grosclaude" %}


Title: Diversity, Key to Survival in the Face of Climate Change and Cultural Cooling

David Grosclaude, a journalist and Occitan activist, has published an essay titled "Diversity for Survival" published by ADEO. This book establishes a strong link between linguistic diversity and biodiversity, two issues closely related.

Grosclaude emphasizes that the loss of minority languages is a threat to biodiversity because traditional linguistic communities often have a close relationship with their environment and cultural practices. He highlights examples of regions where the preservation of linguistic diversity has contributed to protecting local biodiversity, such as the Amazon or the Pyrenees.

The author insists that linguistic diversity is an important resource for society and that it must be protected in the same way as biodiversity. He proposes concrete measures to promote linguistic diversity, such as teaching minority languages in schools, promoting local culture, and supporting initiatives to revitalize endangered languages.

Finally, Grosclaude emphasizes that the preservation of linguistic diversity is a major challenge for our society, but it can also be a source of innovation and creativity. He calls for a holistic approach to climate change and cultural cooling that takes into account linguistic diversity as a key element for our survival and progress.

### Roland Garros 2026: What does the rules say about toilet breaks for players?


The 2026 Roland Garros rules specify the conditions for player breaks. According to this document, each player is authorized to take a break during a match of two sets or more, with the approval of their opponent and under the supervision of an umpire. This break must be taken between games and cannot last longer than 90 seconds. In the event of a dispute between players regarding the need or duration of the break, the umpire has the power to make a decision. Furthermore, if a player refuses to allow their opponent to take a permitted break, they may be penalized by the umpire.
