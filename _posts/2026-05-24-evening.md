### Mintrastu / Wild mint

    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/ddc9acf0-3fef-4dec-87c3-491b8418c1be/464x261_sc_menthe-mintrastu.jpg"
    alt="Mintrastu / Wild mint"
    caption="U mintrastu / La menthe sauvage" %}


Menthol is an aromatic compound extracted from the plant Mentha arvensis, also known as wild mint. This plant is native to Central Asia and Eastern Europe, but it has been introduced to other parts of the world due to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Menthol is used in many products such as cigarettes, chewing gum, e-cigarettes, toothpastes, lotions, and cosmetics for its fresh flavor and decongestant effect. It is also used in some medications to relieve coughs, muscle pain, and headaches.

Menthol has a local effect on the receptors of sensitive nerves, which causes a sensation of freshness and a decongestant effect in the nose. However, it can also cause skin or respiratory irritation in some people.

The production of menthol is mainly concentrated in Southeast Asia, including China, Indonesia, and Thailand. Most of the menthol produced is used in the manufacture of cigarettes, but its increasing demand in other sectors such as health and beauty has led to an increase in production.

In conclusion, menthol is an aromatic compound extracted from wild mint, which is used in many products due to its medicinal and aromatic properties. It is mainly produced in Southeast Asia, and its increasing demand in other sectors has led to an increase in production.
