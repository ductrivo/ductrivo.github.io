### Ban on leaving France, psychological care: what obligations are imposed on Patrick Bruel?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/e6401b4d-eacc-4e15-911d-60010c50cd2e/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Ban on leaving France, psychological care: what obligations are imposed on Patrick Bruel?"
    caption="Interdiction de quitter la France, soins psychologiques : quelles sont les obligations imposées à Patrick Bruel ?" %}


French singer Patrick Bruel, who was placed under investigation on Wednesday for rape, attempted rape, sexual assault, and sexual harassment, has been released after his detention. He is now subject to judicial control, which imposes specific obligations on him.

Bruel is prohibited from leaving France without prior authorization from the investigating judge. Furthermore, he must undergo psychological treatment and refrain from any professional activity that could endanger people or cause public disturbances.

In addition, Bruel is required to respect a ban on approaching the victim and must regularly report to the police. He is also required to provide biological samples when requested by the judicial authorities.

Finally, Bruel must refrain from any contact with witnesses or individuals who may have been present during the alleged incident. He is also prohibited from destroying, altering, or concealing any evidence that could be useful to the investigation.

These obligations are imposed on Bruel as part of the judicial process and can be modified or removed by the investigating judge depending on the progress of the investigation.

### 'In a few years, the CNRS will be a nursing home': Researchers in Grenoble go on strike against budget cuts


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/b0685174-a09c-4ced-8d0f-6c1bcfcf0f5d/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="'In a few years, the CNRS will be a nursing home': Researchers in Grenoble go on strike against budget cuts"
    caption="'Dans quelques années, le CNRS sera un Ehpad' :  des chercheurs en grève à Grenoble contre des coupes budgétaires" %}


Researchers and staff from the National Center for Scientific Research (CNRS) held a protest in Grenoble on Thursday, June 11, to protest against threatened budget cuts for research. Approximately 150 people gathered on the Presqu'île of Grenoble, denouncing, among other things, a freeze on the hiring of fixed-term contracts (CDD) for 2026. The protesters also stated that these measures could compromise the quality of research and put ongoing projects at risk. They also expressed their concern about the possibility that the CNRS could become a residential care facility for elderly and dependent people in a few years, which would be a radical transformation of the institution. The protesters called on authorities to review their budget policy and ensure the continuation of scientific research in France.

### Drôme. Nuclear: He is demanding compensation for the proximity of his commune to a nuclear power plant.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://cdn-s-www.ledauphine.com/images/3D2D0FB0-34FC-4959-881D-10366D243EFD/NW_listB/nucleaire-il-demande-une-indemnite-pour-la-proximite-de-sa-commune-avec-une-centrale-1781196208.jpg"
    alt="Drôme. Nuclear: He is demanding compensation for the proximity of his commune to a nuclear power plant."
    caption="Drôme. Nucléaire : il demande une indemnité pour la proximité de sa commune avec une centrale" %}


The municipality of Tourrettes, located in the Drôme department, has raised a sensitive issue by requesting compensation for its proximity to the Cruas-Meysse nuclear power plant. This request is both unusual and legitimate, as neighboring municipalities have benefited from specific subsidies related to their proximity to the plant, but Tourrettes has not received such aid.

Félix Mercier, a former leader of the local opposition, has written a letter to the prefecture to alert them to this situation. He emphasizes that the subsidies distributed to neighboring municipalities are intended to compensate for the potential negative effects of the nuclear power plant on their environment and populations, but that Tourrettes has been excluded from this scheme.

This situation has caused some concern in the municipality of Tourrettes, which wants to be treated fairly in terms of subsidies related to its proximity to the nuclear power plant.

### Anger is brewing


Anger is an intense emotion that can be triggered by various events or situations. It is characterized by an increase in voice tone, agitated movements, aggressive thoughts, and a feeling of warmth in the body. Anger can have different causes, such as frustration, injustice, fear, or pain. It can also be due to psychological factors such as past traumas or personal tendencies.

Anger is a normal and natural emotion that can serve as a stimulus for taking positive actions, such as defending oneself or others. However, it can also become destructive if not properly managed. The consequences of anger can range from simple stress to aggressive behavior and property damage.

It is important to learn how to manage anger to avoid negative consequences. Techniques such as deep breathing, physical exercise, meditation, or decision-making can help reduce tension and calm anger. It is also important to communicate with others to resolve conflicts and avoid situations that can trigger anger.

Finally, it is important to remember that anger is not an emotion to be completely avoided, but rather an emotion to be managed to protect oneself and others.
