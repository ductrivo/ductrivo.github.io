### Why are cannonballs decorating the facades of Old Lille?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/07b4ede0-d07d-434c-beb0-4823ec43e451/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Why are cannonballs decorating the facades of Old Lille?"
    caption="Pourquoi des boulets de canon décorent les façades du Vieux-Lille ?" %}


In the Old Town of Lille, some facades still bear the marks of a forgotten siege that took place in 1792. At that time, the city was subjected to intense bombardment by over 30,000 Austrian cannonballs. Today, these projectiles have been transformed into decorative elements. Stéphane Bern recounts how some of them have become architectural features, integrated into the walls of historic buildings to recall the city's tumultuous history.

*[Source](https://www.ici.fr/emissions/stephane-bern-voyage-dans-le-temps/pourquoi-des-boulets-de-canon-decorent-les-facades-du-vieux-lille-1253990?at_medium=lien_RSS&at_locale=29)*
