### Garden diseases: how to prevent them?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/06/8b254cd4-2e59-452d-a9b3-e6a8b224827a/464x261_sc_gettyimages-629647243.jpg"
    alt="Garden diseases: how to prevent them?"
    caption="Les maladies au jardin : comment s'en prémunir ?" %}


Title: Preventing Diseases in the Garden: Practical Tips

Despite careful attention to your garden, an unexpected storm or unclean tools can cause strange symptoms in your plants and cause them to lose their luster. To minimize losses, Bastien Glomot, nurseryman-landscape designer, offers you some practical tips.

1. Regular Cleaning: Regularly cleaning your tools and hands before working in the garden can prevent the spread of diseases.
2. Remove Waste: Remove dead plant debris from the soil to prevent diseases from developing.
3. Plant Selection: Choose disease-resistant plants that are suitable for your region.
4. Plant Spacing: Respect the recommended spacing between your plants to allow for good air circulation and prevent pest infestations.
5. Regular Treatment: Apply an antiparasitic treatment regularly to prevent diseases.
6. Remove Pests: Remove pests such as snails, ants, and spiders that can harm your plants.
7. Soil Selection: Choose well-drained soil to prevent water stagnation and the spread of diseases.
8. Remove Existing Diseases: Remove existing diseases using specific products or by seeking the help of a professional.
9. Regularly Clean the Soil: Regularly clean the soil around your plants to prevent the spread of diseases.
10. Remove Weeds: Remove weeds that can absorb nutrients from plants and make them vulnerable to diseases.

By following these tips, you can prevent diseases in your garden and keep your plants healthy.

### Bernadette Chirac, the former First Lady, has died at the age of 93.


Bernadette Chirac, the former First Lady of France, has died at the age of 93. She was the wife of Jacques Chirac, who was President of the French Republic from 1995 to 2007. Bernadette Chirac was born in 1924 and married Jacques Chirac in 1962. She accompanied him throughout his political career, notably when he was mayor of Paris from 1977 to 1995. Bernadette Chirac was known for her commitment to humanitarian and environmental causes. She also wrote several books on the history of France and her personal memories. She died on April 28, 2023, at her home in Brittany, surrounded by her family.
