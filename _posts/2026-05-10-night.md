### Abandoned or seized turtles in Drôme: 'We cannot respond to the request'


{% include figure.html
    src="https://cdn-s-www.ledauphine.com/images/F9B8C43A-C0A4-4BFD-8292-31C302E8832A/NW_listB/tortues-abandonnees-ou-saisies-par-les-autorites-on-ne-peut-pas-repondre-a-la-demande-1778434344.jpg"
    alt="Abandoned or seized turtles in Drôme: 'We cannot respond to the request'"
    caption="Drôme. Tortues abandonnées ou saisies par les autorités : « On ne peut pas répondre à la demande »" %}


The department of Drôme is facing a growing problem of abandoned or illegally seized turtles by authorities. The Zoo d'Upie, located in this region, has been flooded with requests to house these turtles as private owners. However, the establishment can no longer accept new animals due to regulatory and technical constraints.

Local authorities have reported an increase in abandoned or illegally seized turtles, which has led to a strong demand for them to be placed with private individuals who are raising these animals in violation of the law. The Zoo d'Upie, unable to accept new animals, has decided to raise the alarm and wishes to sensitize the public about this problem.

The zoo calls on citizens to be aware of the consequences of abandoning or illegally seizing animals, particularly for turtles that need a specific environment and appropriate care to survive. The Zoo d'Upie also wants to encourage citizens to adopt animals while respecting the laws and regulatory standards, as well as to support initiatives aimed at protecting turtles in their natural habitat.
