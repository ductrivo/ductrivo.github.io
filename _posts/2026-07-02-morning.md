### Why isn't the Maison Carrée in Nîmes square?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/1913f93e-43f1-434f-87fb-20054b469703/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Why isn't the Maison Carrée in Nîmes square?"
    caption="Pourquoi la Maison Carrée de Nîmes n’est pas carrée ?" %}


The most famous building in Nîmes, the Maison Carrée, is a Roman building that does not live up to its name. Although the term "square" suggests a perfectly geometric structure, this building is not really square. This peculiarity is due to the evolution of the meaning of the word over the centuries. Initially, the word "square" referred to a rectangular building with equal sides, but later it was used to describe any rectangular building, even if it was not perfectly square. The Maison Carrée is therefore an example of this linguistic evolution. However, despite its misleading name, the Maison Carrée remains a remarkable example of Roman architecture.

*[Source](https://www.ici.fr/emissions/stephane-bern-voyage-dans-le-temps/pourquoi-la-maison-carree-de-nimes-n-est-pas-carree-7346137?at_medium=lien_RSS&at_locale=29)*
