### Departing on vacation: very heavy traffic on Friday and Saturday in the direction of departures. How to avoid traffic jams?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/d23c7f3c-9ec5-4d42-b237-dab7cb5853c9/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Departing on vacation: very heavy traffic on Friday and Saturday in the direction of departures. How to avoid traffic jams?"
    caption="Départs en vacances : circulation très dense vendredi et samedi dans le sens des départs, comment éviter les bouchons ?" %}


Departures for vacation are scheduled for Friday and Saturday, resulting in heavy traffic in the direction of departures. Traffic will be particularly heavy in eastern France on Friday and throughout France on Saturday. Many families are traveling to their vacation destinations, which further complicates the situation. Here are some tips to avoid getting stuck in traffic:

1. Plan your departure as early as possible, before traffic becomes too heavy.
2. Use alternative routes if possible, avoiding the most congested roads.
3. Check road conditions and traffic information before you leave using navigation apps or local radio.
4. If you have the choice, avoid traveling during the weekend to reach your destination more quickly.
5. If you are traveling with family, try to use multiple cars if possible, to reduce traffic congestion.
6. If you are delayed, don't panic and try to stay calm. It may be helpful to make regular stops to rest and relax.

*[Source](https://www.ici.fr/infos/transports/departs-en-vacances-circulation-dense-vendredi-et-samedi-dans-le-sens-des-departs-comment-eviter-les-bouchons-1886474?at_medium=lien_RSS&at_locale=29)*


### Drought: Several areas in the Isère department are entering a state of heightened alert, with water restrictions becoming stricter.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/5c88704f-816a-4ef7-9113-213047a63953/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Drought: Several areas in the Isère department are entering a state of heightened alert, with water restrictions becoming stricter."
    caption="Sécheresse : plusieurs secteurs de l'Isère passent en alerte renforcée, les restrictions d'eau s'accentuent" %}


The persistent drought is affecting several sectors of the Isère department, leading to an increase in strengthened alerts. The affected areas include the Chartreuse, the Paladru lake, the Bièvre, the Trièves, and the Matheysine. Restrictions on water use are becoming more common in response to the growing demand. The prefecture of the department has taken measures to limit water use, including banning non-essential watering and recommending reasonable consumption of drinking water. The authorities have also called for citizens to be vigilant and to show solidarity in reducing their water consumption in order to help combat the effects of the drought.

*[Source](https://www.ici.fr/auvergne-rhone-alpes/isere-38/grenoble/secheresse-plusieurs-secteurs-passent-en-alerte-renforcee-les-restrictions-s-accentuent-2629263?at_medium=lien_RSS&at_locale=29)*

### More than 500,000 people without electricity in Chile after a violent storm, the strongest in 20 years.

Authorities have declared a state of emergency to manage the situation and provide aid to affected populations. Repair teams are being deployed to restore power as quickly as possible, but difficult weather conditions may delay the work. Those affected have been asked to take precautions to avoid any risk of injury or further damage.
