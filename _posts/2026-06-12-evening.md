The Drôme department has seen a significant development in the Crépol case: the prosecution has requested that eleven defendants be tried in the juvenile court. This decision comes two and a half years after the death of Thomas Perotto, which has caused great emotion throughout the country.

This case is considered exceptional due to its particularly violent and complex nature. The defendants are suspected of having committed acts of violence and harassment against Thomas Perotto, who was ultimately killed.

Investigators have worked hard to establish the facts and identify the individuals involved in this tragedy. The evidence gathered has shown that several individuals played an active role in the death of Thomas Perotto.

The prosecution has therefore requested that these eleven defendants be tried in the juvenile court, which is specially competent for cases involving minors. This decision marks a new stage in this case, which continues to attract a great deal of interest and concern throughout the country.

The next hearings will need to determine the guilt or innocence of the defendants and to bring justice to the memory of Thomas Perotto.

### Who is Mourad Farès, the former recruiter for jihad in Syria who was stripped of his French nationality?


Mourad Farès is a well-known figure for having been a key recruiter for jihad in Syria. He lost his French nationality after being convicted in absentia in France for accusations of terrorism and recruiting fighters for the Islamic State (IS).

Born in 1983 in Paris, Mourad Farès grew up in the French suburbs. He became a radicalized militant in his adolescence and was convicted in France for crimes related to terrorism in 2005. Despite this, he was able to leave the country in 2013 and settle in Syria, where he participated in the civil war as a recruiter for IS.

In 2014, Mourad Farès was identified by French intelligence services as one of the main perpetrators of recruiting French fighters for IS. He was also accused of organizing terrorist attacks in France and Europe.

In 2015, Mourad Farès was convicted in absentia in France of a 18-year prison sentence for his terrorist activities. He also lost his French nationality due to his crimes. Since then, he has been considered an international fugitive and is being sought by French and European authorities.

Mourad Farès is an example of extreme radicalization that can lead to terrorist activities and the loss of French nationality. He remains a threat to French and international national security.
