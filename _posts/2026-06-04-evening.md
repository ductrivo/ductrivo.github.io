### Disappearance of Marjane Satrapi: How the artist revolutionized comic books with 'Persepolis'


Title: Disappearance of Marjane Satrapi: How the artist revolutionized comics with "Persepolis"

Marjane Satrapi, born in Iran in 1969 and emigrated to France at the age of 10, is an artist known for her work in comics. Her most famous work is "Persepolis", published for the first time in 2000.

"Persepolis" is an autobiographical story that tells the childhood and adolescence of Marjane in a post-revolutionary Iran, marked by the Iran-Iraq War and religious restrictions. The book was praised for its unique graphic style, its sincerity, and its ability to offer a personal perspective on contemporary Iran.

The comic has achieved great commercial and critical success, winning numerous international awards, including the Prix du Livre Inter and the Renaudot Prize in 2003. It was also adapted into a film by Marjane Satrapi herself in 2007.

"Persepolis" has had a significant impact on the comic book industry, as it has given a voice to people and events that would otherwise not have been addressed in this medium. It has also contributed to popularizing comics as a serious art form and to opening up the debate on political and social issues in the world.

Marjane Satrapi is still active in the field of comics, regularly publishing books and collaborating with magazines and newspapers. She is considered one of the most important contemporary comic artists.
