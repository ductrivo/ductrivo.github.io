Heat alert extended to 18 departments in France, including Paris and its inner suburbs. Météo France has extended the yellow heat alert level until Monday, May 23rd. This represents an early and remarkable heatwave that affects the west of the country, including Paris itself. Temperatures are expected to be higher than average for this time of year, with a risk of temperatures rising to 30°C in some regions. Citizens are therefore advised to remain vigilant and to take the necessary precautions to avoid the harmful effects of the heat, including protecting themselves from the sun, staying hydrated, and ensuring that no one is isolated.

### Women's Tour de France: discover the profiles and routes of the 9 stages.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://cdn-s-www.ledauphine.com/images/B3953251-320A-4189-A16F-6B2368FDFE99/NW_listB/tour-de-france-femmes-decouvrez-les-profils-et-parcours-des-9-etapes-1779653052.jpg"
    alt="Women's Tour de France: discover the profiles and routes of the 9 stages."
    caption="Cyclisme. Tour de France femmes : découvrez les profils et parcours des 9 étapes" %}


The Women's Tour de France will take place from Saturday, August 1st to Sunday, August 9th. The race will start in Lausanne and finish in Nice, covering a nine-stage course.

Stage 1 will be a 10.4 km road race around Lausanne. Stage 2, 137.6 km long, will link Lausanne to Le Havre. Stage 3, 158.9 km long, will take the riders from Le Havre to Saint-Lô.

Stage 4 is a queen stage with a circuit around Fougères and two major climbs: the Côte de la Fosse and the Col du Granit. Stage 5, 126 km long, will link Fougères to Châteauroux.

Stage 6 is a queen stage with a circuit around Saint-Amand-Montrond and two major climbs: the Col de la Croix Neuve and the Col de la Loze. Stage 7, 105 km long, will link Saint-Amand-Montrond to La Rochelle.

Stage 8 will be a 96 km road race around La Rochelle. Stage 9, 127.6 km long, will take the riders from La Rochelle to Nice.

Key climbs may include the Côte de la Fosse, the Col du Granit, the Col de la Croix Neuve, and the Col de la Loze. Full profiles of the stages and climbs are available on the official website of the Women's Tour de France.
