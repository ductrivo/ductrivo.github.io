### 30% of the archives have been cleaned: The historical archives of Brest, which were closed for two years after a fire, are reopening.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/73fbc8c8-ab84-4463-bbbf-77e21090a77c/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="30% of the archives have been cleaned: The historical archives of Brest, which were closed for two years after a fire, are reopening."
    caption="30 % des archives nettoyées : le Service historique de la Défense de Brest rouvre ses portes deux ans après un incendie" %}


The Defense Historical Service (SHD) in Brest reopened its doors on July 12, two years after a fire that had damaged its archives building. Among the documents preserved, 30% have been cleaned and are now available to the public in the SHD reading room. The fire, which broke out in July 2019, caused significant damage to the Brest military archives. Despite the damage, a large part of the documents were able to be saved and restored. The SHD is a French public institution that preserves and makes available to the public documents relating to the history of the French military.

*[Source](https://www.ici.fr/bretagne/finistere-29/brest/30-des-archives-nettoyees-le-service-historique-de-la-defense-de-brest-rouvre-ses-portes-deux-ans-apres-un-incendie-8160479?at_medium=lien_RSS&at_locale=29)*

### This 5,000-year-old site is one of the most mysterious in Normandy… and yet, very few visitors know about it!


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/25b09ea4-6363-4623-876f-6910f1f8613f/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="This 5,000-year-old site is one of the most mysterious in Normandy… and yet, very few visitors know about it!"
    caption="Ce site vieux de 5 000 ans est l'un des plus mystérieux de Normandie… et pourtant, peu de visiteurs le connaissent !" %}


This archaeological site, located in the department of Orne in Normandy, has a history dating back 5,000 years. Despite its interest and mysterious atmosphere, it remains relatively unknown to visitors. Perched on the heights of Merri, the Camp de Bierre is one of the most impressive archaeological sites in western France. The monumental remains testify to the presence of the first inhabitants of Normandy. The preserved nature surrounding the site offers a pleasant walk for visitors. During the summer, activities are organized to make the discovery of the site more attractive.

### 'High risk of mental health problems': towards a ban on social media for European teenagers?


The current debate centers on the possibility of banning minors in Europe from accessing social media due to high risks of mental health problems. Experts argue that young users are exposed to inappropriate content, cyberbullying, and digital addiction, which can negatively impact their mental well-being.

European authorities have become aware of these issues and have begun to take measures to address them. For example, some platforms have been implemented to filter inappropriate content, and authorities have also enacted laws to regulate the use of social media by minors.

However, some experts believe that these measures are not sufficient and that a complete ban on minors accessing social media would be necessary to protect their mental health. They argue that most minors have access to the internet via their smartphones, making it difficult to control their use of social media.

Other experts believe that such a ban would be excessive and that young people should be encouraged to use social media responsibly. They argue that these platforms can also offer benefits to young people, such as the ability to communicate with others and share their creativity.

In conclusion, the debate on banning social media for minors in Europe is ongoing. Experts are divided on whether this measure would be necessary to protect the mental health of young people or whether it would be excessive and unnecessary.
