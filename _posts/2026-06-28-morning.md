1. Create a budget: This is a plan that allows you to know your monthly expenses and income, as well as the financial goals you want to achieve.
2. Compare bank offers: To find the best bank product that suits your needs, it is important to compare the offers of different banks based on their interest rates, fees, and opening conditions.
3. Avoid pitfalls: Some bank products can be a source of financial losses. For example, variable-rate loans can become expensive if interest rates rise. It is therefore important to understand the terms of the products before purchasing them.
4. Take advantage of assistance: Some banks offer free services or financial assistance to help individuals better manage their finances. For example, the Bank of France offers a financial support service that allows individuals to receive personalized assistance to better understand their financial situation and find suitable solutions.

In conclusion, this text offers useful advice for better managing one's finances by creating a budget, comparing bank offers, avoiding pitfalls, and taking advantage of the financial assistance offered by some banks.
