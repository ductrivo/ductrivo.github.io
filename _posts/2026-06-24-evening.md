In the villages of the Drôme, the struggle to maintain coolness is a daily battle against the intense heat that makes people and homes drowsy. Despite their familiarity with the heat, the residents demonstrate a resilience comparable to that of urban dwellers. They leave their homes early in the morning to enjoy the fresh air, and then retreat during the scorching midday hours.

The measures taken to combat the heat include the use of awnings and damp sheets to cool the rooms, as well as the removal of organic waste to prevent it from fermenting and producing an unpleasant odor. Children play in the streams and fountains, while adults seek refuge under trees or in churches to find some shade.

Farmers are particularly affected by the heat, as it threatens their cereal and fruit crops. They must therefore work early in the morning to avoid the hottest hours, and seek to protect their production from the harmful effects of the heat.

Finally, local authorities have implemented measures to help the most vulnerable populations, such as opening shelters for the homeless and distributing refreshing drinks to those in need.

In conclusion, despite the intense heat that makes the villages of the Drôme drowsy, the residents demonstrate great resilience by using practical measures to maintain coolness and protect their health.
