### Did the ball touch a cable? We asked a physicist to analyze the controversial goal awarded to England.


On December 18th, a controversial goal was scored by the England team during the World Cup semi-final against Croatia. Rumors circulated that the ball had hit an electrical wire before crossing the goal line, which would have invalidated the goal. To determine the truth, an analysis was requested from a physicist.

The analysis revealed that the ball had not hit an electrical wire before crossing the goal line. In fact, post-match footage showed that the ball was far enough from the cable to not touch it. Furthermore, there was no visual or audio evidence of the ball hitting an electrical wire.

However, the rumors continued to circulate and were even picked up by some media outlets. This led to a strong reaction from English fans, who accused the Croatians of cheating. The FIFA authorities also denied these rumors and confirmed that the goal was valid.

This case highlighted the importance of verifying information before spreading it, as well as the need for an objective analysis to establish the truth in controversial situations.

*[Source](https://www.leparisien.fr/sports/football/coupe-du-monde/le-ballon-a-t-il-touche-un-cable-on-a-demande-a-un-physicien-danalyser-le-but-polemique-accorde-a-langleterre-12-07-2026-DODD4C2JJRHZBFYK3FKIIRSQPQ.php)*
