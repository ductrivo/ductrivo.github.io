### N as in Negresco: the secret history of the legendary palace


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/fc747485-833c-4a38-ad27-1353536c96e2/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="N as in Negresco: the secret history of the legendary palace"
    caption="N comme Negresco : l’histoire secrète du palace mythique" %}


The Negresco, in Nice, is an iconic palace since its construction in 1913. Its white exterior and pink dome are symbols of the city. Over the years, the palace has combined history, luxury, art, celebrities, and unusual anecdotes.

The palace was built by Henri Negresco, a Romanian hotel entrepreneur, who also founded the Hotel de la Plage in Cannes. The Negresco quickly became a meeting place for celebrities and political figures of the time.

During World War II, the palace was occupied by the Germans, who used its rooms for meetings and official events. After the war, the Negresco continued to host celebrities such as Charlie Chaplin, Marlene Dietrich, and Pablo Picasso.

In 1957, Henri Negresco died, and his son, Jean-Jacques, took over. He continued to develop the palace by adding art and organizing cultural events.

In 1992, the Negresco was bought by the French businessman Jean-Luc Poujol. He invested in the restoration of the palace and added new services such as conference rooms and a spa.

Today, the Negresco is still a meeting place for celebrities and influential people. It is also known for its art collection, which includes more than 300 works by painters such as Matisse, Chagall, and Dali. The palace continues to be a symbol of luxury and prestige in Nice.

### Scissors effect: Is your company affected? How to avoid falling victim to the cost explosion


The "scissors effect," or "pliers effect," refers to an economic situation in which the costs of three factors of production – raw materials, labor, and financial services – increase simultaneously. This situation can have a negative impact on the affected businesses, leading to increased production costs and reduced profit margins.

To avoid the negative impact of rising costs, it is important for businesses to adopt certain strategies:

1. Cost optimization: Businesses can seek to reduce production costs by optimizing the use of raw materials and by finding more competitive suppliers.
2. Product diversification: Businesses can diversify their products to meet different market segments, which can help to avoid concentrating costs on a single product.
3. Technological innovation: Businesses can invest in research and development of new technologies to improve their efficiency and reduce their production costs.
4. Risk management: Businesses must also consider the risks associated with the scissors effect in their strategic planning and seek to minimize these risks by using appropriate risk management tools.
5. Cooperation with partners: Businesses can collaborate with their partners (suppliers, customers, etc.) to reduce costs and improve their efficiency.
6. Geographical diversification: Businesses can also diversify their activities in different regions of the world to meet different markets and reduce their dependence on a single country or region.
