### Child protection: staff members with inappropriate behavior at school will be listed on a 'blacklist'


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/8d2682e5-29c0-4066-8633-19d690a00465/140x140_sc_maxnewsfrsix252886.jpg"
    alt="Child protection: staff members with inappropriate behavior at school will be listed on a 'blacklist'"
    caption="Protection de l'enfance : les personnels aux comportements inappropriés à l'école seront inscrits sur une 'liste noire'" %}


The French Minister of Education, Edouard Geffray, announced on February 24 a draft law aimed at protecting children in schools. Staff members who have engaged in inappropriate behavior with minors will now be listed on a blacklist. This measure is intended to strengthen the protection of students and to put an end to all forms of harassment or sexual abuse in schools. Individuals listed on this blacklist will be prohibited from exercising their professional activities in education. The draft law must be examined and approved by the French Parliament.

    caption="Immobilier. Le marché du neuf fragilisé, l'ancien en progression, redynamiser les centres-villes : les notaires tirent leur bilan" %}


The French real estate market is currently characterized by fragility in the new construction sector, while the existing market is strengthening. Notaries in the Drôme, Isère, and Hautes-Alpes regions recently held a meeting to discuss the current situation of their profession and the difficult real estate market.

The notaries have observed that the new construction market is fragile due to a decline in sales, particularly in city centers. This can be attributed to a crisis of confidence among buyers in the face of rising prices and economic uncertainties.

Despite this, the existing real estate market is on the rise, with a revitalization of city centers. The notaries have pointed out that this trend may be due to increasing demand for properties in these areas, as well as to the increase in property prices on the outskirts.

The notaries also discussed the need to revitalize city centers to attract more buyers and strengthen the new construction market. They proposed several measures, including the development of attractive commercial and residential areas, as well as policies for the rehabilitation of historic buildings.

In conclusion, the notaries have highlighted the current fragility of the French new construction real estate market, but have also emphasized the revitalization of the existing market and the need to revitalize city centers to attract more buyers.

### Beausemblant. A third 5G antenna, this time planned by Orange, is causing controversy.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://cdn-s-www.ledauphine.com/images/C7B79BDB-C614-4C28-AF42-B08C212298F8/NW_listB/une-troisieme-antenne-5g-cette-fois-prevue-par-orange-fait-debat-1779887978.jpg"
    alt="Beausemblant. A third 5G antenna, this time planned by Orange, is causing controversy."
    caption="Beausemblant. Une troisième antenne 5G, cette fois prévue par Orange, fait débat" %}


Beausemblant: A debate over a new 5G antenna by Orange

In the village of Beausemblant, the installation of a third 5G antenna by Orange is causing controversy. The municipal opposition is questioning the absolute necessity of this antenna, as the operator SFR already has an antenna located approximately 100 meters away.

Orange has made an offer to buy out Bouygues and Free, which could lead to changes in the telecommunications landscape within the village. However, local authorities want the potential impact on human health and the environment to be assessed before any final decision is made.

Opponents of this antenna have mobilized to demand a thorough study of the potential effects of the radiation emitted by 5G antennas on health and the environment, as well as to express their concerns about the implications of this project on the diversity of telecommunications operators in the village.

Supporters of the installation of the antenna highlight the potential benefits for residents in terms of connectivity and data speed, as well as the investment and job creation opportunities that could result.

The project is currently awaiting a final decision from local authorities, who must take into account the concerns of citizens while evaluating the potential benefits for the village.
