### Caves Byrrh in Thuir: the largest wine cellar and the largest oak vat in the world!


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/e3630cbb-1626-4ac3-b8d6-e0f259414d61/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Caves Byrrh in Thuir: the largest wine cellar and the largest oak vat in the world!"
    caption="Caves Byrrh à Thuir : la plus grande cave et la plus grande cuve en chêne du monde !" %}


The Byrrh Caves, located in Thuir, are distinguished by their exceptional size: they have the largest cave and the largest oak vat in the world. Located just a few minutes from Perpignan, these caves offer a unique experience. They are not just a place to visit, but rather a journey through time and history, where you can discover the industrial heritage and the flavors of the Roussillon. The Byrrh Caves were created in 1863 by two brothers, Joseph and Jean-François Père, who began producing a unique fortified wine called Byrrh. Today, the caves are open to the public for guided tours where you can discover the history of the brand and the process of making their iconic product. The Byrrh Caves also offer an exhibition space dedicated to the history of the region, as well as a gourmet restaurant where you can taste the traditional flavors of the Roussillon.
