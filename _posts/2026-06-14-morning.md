The consumption of products such as honey, more fruit-based jams, fruit juice, and powdered milk is regulated by a European directive known as the "breakfast" directive, which is now being implemented in France. This rule will come into effect this Sunday. It requires clearer labeling of the composition of these products for consumers. Specifically, labels must now indicate more precisely the ingredients used in each product. This measure aims to improve transparency and understanding of product information for French consumers.

Title: Reading Ideas for Summer for Gardening Enthusiasts

Summer is a time for relaxation, and reading can be a pleasant way to enjoy it. Here are some interesting book suggestions for gardening enthusiasts:

1. "The Theory of the Garden" by Albert Speer: This book covers the fundamental principles of garden design and landscaping, as well as their history.
2. "The Secret Garden" by Andrei Molotiu: This book presents a collection of photos of secret and intimate gardens from around the world.
3. "Inspirational Gardens" by Nigel Dunnett and James Hitchmough: This book offers original ideas for creating sustainable and eco-friendly gardens, using resilient and locally adapted plants.
4. "The Romantic Gardener" by William Robinson: Published in 1883, this book is considered a classic in gardening literature. It covers the fundamental principles of designing and landscaping romantic gardens.
5. "The Eden Gardener" by Christopher Lloyd: This book presents the advice and tips of the famous British gardener for creating remarkable gardens.
6. "Terroir Gardens" by Luc Schusterman: This book covers the fundamental principles of garden design based on the local terrain, history, and culture.
7. "The Solitary Gardener" by Michel de Montaigne: Published in 1580, this book is considered a classic in gardening literature. It covers the thoughts and reflections of the French philosopher on his own gardening experience.

These books can be a source of inspiration for gardening enthusiasts who are looking to improve their skills in garden design and landscaping, as well as to discover original ideas for creating remarkable green spaces.

### Sedum, sempervivum, lewisia, gaura, and dipladenia: drought-resistant plants


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/353ecd87-b3ca-414a-9ee3-732b9fb725f2/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Sedum, sempervivum, lewisia, gaura, and dipladenia: drought-resistant plants"
    caption="Sedum, sempervivum, lewisia, gaura et dipladenia : des plantes qui résistent à la sécheresse" %}


Drought-tolerant plants such as Sedum, Sempervivum, Lewisia, Gaura, and Dipladenia are interesting options for beautifying a garden while reducing water needs. These species are known for their drought tolerance and ability to survive with minimal care.

Bastien Glomot, a horticulturalist and landscape designer, offers helpful advice on incorporating these plants into a garden. He recommends choosing species that are suitable for the region and local climate. It is also important to plant the plants according to their water needs, in order to avoid areas that are too wet or too dry.

Drought-tolerant plants can be incorporated into gardens of various styles, from Mediterranean to xerophytic. They can also be used to create transition zones between wet and dry areas, providing a practical and aesthetically pleasing solution.

In addition to their drought tolerance, these plants also have the advantage of being generally easy to grow and require minimal maintenance. They are also often attractive to butterflies and bees, which can help to promote biodiversity in the garden.

By incorporating drought-tolerant plants into a garden, a homeowner can reduce their water needs while creating an attractive and sustainable aesthetic.

### De Gaulle, this true strategist


Charles de Gaulle, born on November 22, 1890, in Lille, and died on November 9, 1970, in Colombey-les-Deux-Églises, was a French politician who played a major role in the history of his country.

Graduating from the École spéciale militaire de Saint-Cyr in 1914, he served during World War I and distinguished himself particularly during the Battle of Verdun. He was promoted to brigadier general at the age of 30.

After the war, he continued his military career and became chief of staff of the French armed forces in 1941. However, he opposed the Vichy regime and was arrested by the Germans in 1940. He went into exile in England, where he joined General Giraud to form the National Liberation Committee (CNL).

In 1944, he participated in the liberation of France and was appointed Prime Minister by the Allies. He then founded the Fourth Republic in 1946, but his policies faced internal difficulties, and he resigned in 1946.

In 1958, he was recalled to power to end the Algerian crisis. He established a new constitution, which gave birth to the Fifth French Republic. He remained President of the Republic until 1969.

Charles de Gaulle is considered one of the greatest French statesmen of the 20th century, known for his determination to defend national independence and his opposition to European integration.
