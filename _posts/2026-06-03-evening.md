### Xavier Dupont de Ligonnès Case: 5 minutes to understand how M6 was tricked by a fake priest


The Xavier Dupont de Ligonnès case is a French criminal case that garnered significant media attention in 2011. The subject was accused of murdering his wife, his four children, and his father, and then hiding their bodies in their home in Nantes.

M6, a French television channel, produced a documentary about the case in 2013, titled "Xavier Dupont de Ligonnès: The Murdering Priest". The documentary featured a man who claimed to be a Catholic priest and who had been in contact with Dupont de Ligonnès before the murders. However, it was revealed that the man was not a priest, but an actor hired by the channel to play the role.

This error was discovered when journalists contacted the real Catholic priest who had been in contact with Dupont de Ligonnès before the murders, and who had never met the actor featured in the documentary.

M6 acknowledged its mistake and publicly apologized for broadcasting an inaccurate documentary. The documentary was withdrawn from circulation, and M6 also announced that it would take measures to prevent this from happening again in the future.

This case highlighted the risks of errors and manipulation in television documentaries, as well as the need for media to verify information before broadcasting it.
