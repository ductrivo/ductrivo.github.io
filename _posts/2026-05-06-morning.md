## Isère


### Amazon will invest more than 15 billion euros in France over three years, creating 7,000 permanent jobs.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/3c8e0693-45ef-4927-8b7b-8028d0772ace/140x140_sc_ibpremiumfour670050.jpg"
    alt="Amazon will invest more than 15 billion euros in France over three years, creating 7,000 permanent jobs."
    caption="Amazon va investir plus de 15 milliards d'euros en France sur trois ans, 7.000 emplois en CDI à la clé" %}


Amazon announces that it will invest more than 15 billion euros in France over a three-year period, which should result in the creation of more than 7,000 full-time jobs. The regions affected by these investments include Eure-et-Loir, Oise, and Rhône. The American giant of online sales announced this decision in a statement published Tuesday evening.

### The socialist Guillaume Lissy elected president of Grenoble-Alpes Métropole at the price of an agreement with small communes and center


{% include figure.html
    src="https://france3-regions.franceinfo.fr/image/HcuX9qKAfCOjEoPK6ELxCZF-ycE/930x620/filters:format(webp)/regions/2026/04/30/69f310cdea918088508860.jpg"
    alt="The socialist Guillaume Lissy elected president of Grenoble-Alpes Métropole at the price of an agreement with small communes and center"
    caption="Le socialiste Guillaume Lissy élu président de Grenoble-Alpes Métropole au prix d'un accord avec les petites communes et le centre" %}


Guillaume Lissy, socialist, was elected president of Grenoble-Alpes Métropole on Thursday, 24 March, after receiving 87 votes out of 109. He succeeds Christophe Ferrari, who was not seeking re-election. The agreement was reached with the small communes and the center. His main opponent, Raphaël Guerrero, mayor of Jarrie, also participated in the agreement. The new president is the mayor of PS Seyssinet-Pariset.

{% include figure.html
    src="https://cdn-s-www.ledauphine.com/images/F3CCE0FB-91F0-4010-B05A-0C4EC25791FF/NW_listB/tanneries-roux-thibaut-monnier-denonce-un-cout-du-travail-devenu-insoutenable-en-france-1778004282.jpg"

### Drôme. 'It's rhythmic but it's enriching': a day in the shoes of a new mayor


{% include figure.html
    src="https://cdn-s-www.ledauphine.com/images/960A2F77-E755-4B5F-B008-F4D3B1DEBC6F/NW_listB/c-est-rythme-mais-c-est-enrichissant-une-journee-dans-la-peau-d-un-nouveau-maire-1778005010.jpg"
    alt="Drôme. 'It's rhythmic but it's enriching': a day in the shoes of a new mayor"
    caption="Drôme. « C’est rythmé mais c’est enrichissant » : une journée dans la peau d'un nouveau maire" %}


The mayor of Valherbasse, located in the department of Drôme, is described as rhythmic but enriching. Vincent Bret was recently elected and opened the doors of the town hall on May 4 for a day immersed in his daily routine. Le Dauphiné libéré followed this rural mayor in his activities.

In the morning, he starts by responding to emails and calling citizens to discuss local concerns. Then, he attends a meeting with school representatives to discuss the school budget. Afterwards, he visits a social housing construction site and meets workers.

After lunch break, he attends a municipal meeting where topics such as territorial planning, municipal budget, and infrastructure projects are addressed. Then, he goes to a local cultural event to support local artists.

Finally, he ends his day by meeting citizens at the town hall to discuss their requests and ideas. The rural mayor is described as being very involved in local life and working hard for the needs of his citizens.

### Moscow intensifies terror: in full ceasefire, Russia continues to strike Ukraine.


Moscow continues its offensive against Ukraine despite the ceasefire: air and ground attacks have been reported in several Ukrainian regions, notably Kharkiv, Mykolaiv, and Mariupol. ### War in Ukraine: Russia could use Algeria as a turning point for its ghost fleet to traffic arms and bypass sanctions.


War in Ukraine: Russia may have a tendency to use Algeria as a rotation point for its ghost fleet for arms trafficking, which would allow it to bypass international sanctions.
