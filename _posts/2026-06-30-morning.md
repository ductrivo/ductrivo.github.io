The bay was named by Greek sailors who discovered it more than 2500 years ago. They named the bay after its resemblance to a bay near the city of Angelos, in Asia Minor. However, this resemblance is not obvious to modern observers.

Baie des Anges is an important historical and cultural site, which has been the scene of many important events in the history of Nice. It is also known for its natural beauty and beaches, which attract thousands of visitors each year.

Today, Baie des Anges is a symbol of the city of Nice and a major tourist attraction. It is also a lively place, with restaurants, bars and shops lining the bay.

In conclusion, the name of Baie des Anges is surprising because it suggests paradise, but its naming has a much more prosaic origin than might be assumed. Despite this, it remains an important historical and cultural site, as well as a major tourist attraction for the city of Nice.

### France: inflation rises to 1.8% year-on-year in June after 2.4% in May


In France, inflation rebounded in June to a rate of 1.8% per year, after reaching 2.4% in the previous month. This increase is mainly due to the prices of fossil fuels and food products. The prices of natural gas and fuel oil have increased by more than 10%, while the prices of vegetables and fruits have increased by 4.5%. In addition, the prices of public transport have also increased by 2.3% compared to the same period the previous year. However, the prices of manufactured goods have fallen by 0.1%, which has helped to limit the overall increase in inflation. Experts believe that this trend could continue in the coming weeks due to the rising prices of fossil fuels and food products, as well as increased production costs.
