### 'I've never been cast in a movie' : the revelations by Manu Payet


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/d19f0707-7aa6-4520-b1ca-e19beddd98d1/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="'I've never been cast in a movie' : the revelations by Manu Payet"
    caption="« Je n’ai jamais été pris sur un casting » : les confidences de Manu Payet" %}


Title: Manu Payet's Confessions about "Become Brilliant"

Manu Payet, a French actor known for his roles in series such as "Call My Agent!" and "Marseille", revealed that he has never been chosen for a casting. In an interview, he shared his experiences filming the heartwarming comedy "Become Brilliant".

The film, shot in Alsace and Germany, features Manu Payet alongside a group of teenagers. The cast tells a deeply human story that revolves around the quest for intelligence and friendship.

Manu Payet shared his memories of the filming, including the difficult moments encountered during the intense emotional scenes. He also revealed that the film was a challenge for him because he had never worked with a group of teenagers before.

In addition to his memories, Manu Payet also shared secrets about the casting process in the film industry. He explained that the casting process can be long and difficult, but that it is important not to lose confidence and to continue to train in order to improve one's chances of being chosen.

Finally, Manu Payet emphasized the importance of the comedy "Become Brilliant" to him, as it allowed him to work with a group of teenagers and share his experience as an actor. He also stated that the film is a call for intelligence and friendship, which can inspire young viewers to pursue their dreams and never lose hope.

### Paris: JR's Caverne is finally back on display after days and nights of work, with an imminent reopening?


The Parisian cultural space known as JR's Cave has finally restored its artwork after several days and nights of work. The reopening is scheduled for soon, although the exact date has not yet been announced. JR's Cave is a popular cultural space located in the Marais district of Paris, which has hosted artists such as Banksy and Invader. The space was closed in 2019 due to safety and renovation concerns. The work involved restoring the exterior and interior walls, as well as improving the site's security. The reopening of JR's Cave will mark its return to the Parisian cultural scene after a two-year absence.
