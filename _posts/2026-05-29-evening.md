### 'A major challenge': the rescue of eight young Americans, trapped upside down on a 30-meter-high amusement park ride.


Title: Rescue Operation for Eight Teenagers Trapped in a Ferris Wheel

On Saturday, May 21st, a rescue team was called in to rescue eight American teenagers who were trapped upside down on a Ferris wheel at a height of thirty meters in an amusement park located in San Antonio, Texas.

The teenagers, aged 12 to 15, had been trapped for over two hours before the rescue services were called. Authorities stated that they were not injured and that their condition was stable.

The rescuers used a mobile platform to access the Ferris wheel and were able to free the teenagers after about two and a half hours of effort. The rescuers also used cutting tools to free the children trapped in the Ferris wheel.

Authorities stated that the incident was due to human error, but that no technical problems had been identified on the Ferris wheel. The eight teenagers were treated by doctors and released after examination.

The amusement park temporarily closed to allow a full investigation of the safety of the Ferris wheel and other attractions. Authorities stated that they would take all necessary measures to prevent such an incident from happening again.
