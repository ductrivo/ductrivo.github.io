### Heatwave: With intense sunshine, how to choose the right sunscreen and the appropriate UV protection factor?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/0af4b89a-dec2-4667-bd8e-d7ef99c548ca/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Heatwave: With intense sunshine, how to choose the right sunscreen and the appropriate UV protection factor?"
    caption="Canicule : avec le fort ensoleillement, comment choisir sa crème solaire et le bon indice UV ?" %}


In June, when the weather is hot and sunny, it is important to protect yourself from the sun's rays and UV radiation to prevent sunburn and the risk of skin cancer. UV radiation is dangerous because it can damage the skin and eyes. To protect yourself, it is recommended to use a sunscreen with an appropriate SPF for your skin type.

There are several types of sunscreens: sunscreens with photoprotection, sunscreens with photostabilizers, and sunscreens with photostimulation. Sunscreens with photoprotection block UVB and UVA rays, while sunscreens with photostabilizers contain ingredients that stabilize the skin against sunlight. Sunscreens with photostimulation increase melanin production in the skin to protect against UV radiation.

It is important to choose a sunscreen with an appropriate SPF based on your skin type. For people with fair skin, it is recommended to use sunscreens with an SPF of 30 or higher. For people with brown or black skin, an SPF of 15 or lower may be sufficient.

It is also important to protect yourself by wearing appropriate clothing, hats, and sunglasses to avoid sun exposure. It is recommended to avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight and to regularly apply sunscreen.

Finally, it is important to know how to read the SPF to choose the right sunscreen. The SPF indicates the amount of UVB radiation that reaches the Earth's surface and varies depending on latitude, time of day, and weather conditions. It is recommended to consult weather forecasts to find out what the SPF is in your area and to choose a sunscreen based on this information.
