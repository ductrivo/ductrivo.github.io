### Drôme - Vaucluse. Tricastin Central: EDF's fault recognized after record contamination of groundwater.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://cdn-s-www.ledauphine.com/images/0B091348-E4E2-4F7C-A5C1-DCF5EFF11425/NW_listB/centrale-du-tricastin-la-faute-d-edf-reconnue-apres-une-contamination-record-des-eaux-souterraines-1782408305.jpg"
    alt="Drôme - Vaucluse. Tricastin Central: EDF's fault recognized after record contamination of groundwater."
    caption="Drôme - Vaucluse. Centrale du Tricastin : la faute d’EDF reconnue après une contamination record des eaux souterraines" %}


The Grenoble court has recognized the civil liability of EDF and its director for an incident at the Tricastin nuclear power plant, located in the departments of Drôme and Vaucluse. In December 2021, a tritium leak caused a record contamination of the site's underground water. The court of appeal ruled that the leak was due to a lack of proper supervision and maintenance by the responsible authorities. This decision marks the first time that EDF has been held liable for contamination of underground water as a result of its nuclear activities in France.

### Heatwave: With intense sunshine, how to choose the right sunscreen and the appropriate UV protection factor?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/0af4b89a-dec2-4667-bd8e-d7ef99c548ca/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Heatwave: With intense sunshine, how to choose the right sunscreen and the appropriate UV protection factor?"
    caption="Canicule : avec le fort ensoleillement, comment choisir sa crème solaire et le bon indice UV ?" %}


In June, when the weather is hot and sunny, it is important to protect yourself from the sun's rays and UV radiation to prevent sunburn and the risk of skin cancer. UV radiation is dangerous because it can damage the skin and eyes. To protect yourself, it is recommended to use a sunscreen with an appropriate SPF for your skin type.

There are several types of sunscreens: sunscreens with photoprotection, sunscreens with photostabilization, and sunscreens with photostimulation. Sunscreens with photoprotection block UVB and UVA rays, while sunscreens with photostabilization contain ingredients that stabilize the skin against sunlight. Sunscreens with photostimulation increase melanin production in the skin to protect against UV radiation.

It is important to choose a sunscreen with an appropriate SPF based on your skin type. For people with fair skin, it is recommended to use sunscreens with an SPF of 30 or higher. For people with brown or black skin, an SPF of 15 or lower may be sufficient.

It is also important to protect yourself by wearing appropriate clothing, hats, and sunglasses to avoid sun exposure. It is recommended to avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight and to regularly apply sunscreen.

Finally, it is important to know how to read the SPF to choose the right sunscreen. The SPF indicates the amount of UVB radiation that reaches the Earth's surface and varies depending on latitude, time of day, and weather conditions. It is recommended to consult weather forecasts to find out what the SPF is in your area and to choose a sunscreen based on this information.

### Heatwaves, heat spikes, and bad weather: how SNCF is adapting its trains to climate change


{% include figure.html
    src="https://france3-regions.franceinfo.fr/image/bd_wMpj0Kb8wnawjYXqis-43fNw/930x620/filters:format(webp)/regions/2025/09/11/15935-68c336cb5d8ba154720995.jpg"
    alt="Heatwaves, heat spikes, and bad weather: how SNCF is adapting its trains to climate change"
    caption="Canicule, pics de chaleurs et intempéries, comment la SNCF adapte ses trains au dérèglement climatique" %}


The heatwave, with episodes of extreme heat and bad weather, is affecting the French railway network and disrupting traffic. The rails can warp due to heat, and the overhead lines can expand. Therefore, the Société Nationale des Chemins de Fer Français (SNCF) must deal with climate-related constraints.

To address these challenges, the SNCF has implemented several measures to adapt to climate change.

First, trains are equipped with cooling systems to maintain a comfortable temperature inside. In addition, regular maintenance is carried out on the rails and overhead lines to prevent them from warping or expanding excessively.

Furthermore, the SNCF has implemented an alert system to warn passengers and employees about the risks associated with heat. Trains are also equipped with ventilation systems to maintain a flow of fresh air in the tunnels.

Finally, the SNCF has developed digital models to predict and anticipate the effects of climate change on the railway network. These models allow for preventative measures to be taken and the network's resilience to extreme weather events to be improved.

In conclusion, the SNCF has implemented several measures to adapt its railway network to climate change and reduce the negative effects of heat on traffic.
