### Flu: The High Council of Health recommends making vaccination mandatory for all healthcare workers.


{% include figure.html

The High Authority for Health recommended on March 1, 2022, that mandatory flu vaccination be implemented for all healthcare professionals. This measure must be approved by a decree from the Council of State to become effective. This decision is being made in the context of the Covid-19 pandemic, which has highlighted the importance of vaccination in protecting patients and healthcare workers. André Weill, from Meylan, recently completed a walking journey from Auschwitz in Poland to Amsterdam in the Netherlands, following in the footsteps of Etty Hillesum, a young Dutch woman deported at the age of 28. He undertook this journey in the opposite direction of Etty Hillesum's fatal route, which she made in September 1943. His goal was to better promote Etty Hillesum's message of peace, which she was killed in Auschwitz. The journey lasted several weeks and allowed André Weill to visit the places where Etty Hillesum lived and worked before her arrest. He also met people who knew Etty Hillesum or were familiar with her writings. André Weill's journey has attracted media attention in France and the Netherlands, and he has been received by the family of Etty Hillesum in Amsterdam.

### 'Injured, disoriented animals...' After the fires, should hunting be banned in the affected areas?


In the context of forest fires, the health status of injured and disoriented animals is a matter of concern. The question then arises as to whether it is appropriate to ban hunting in the affected areas.

There are several reasons to justify such a measure:

1. Injured animals may be more vulnerable to predation or human contact, which can lead to their further death.
2. Hunting can disturb already stressed and disoriented animals, preventing their recovery.
3. Affected areas may be contaminated with toxic substances from the fires, which could have a negative impact on animal health.
4. Hunting can also disrupt ecosystems already weakened by fires, by disrupting animal populations and altering habitats.
5. Finally, banning hunting in affected areas could help to preserve local biodiversity and promote the recovery of ecosystems after fires.

However, it is important to note that banning hunting is not always the ideal solution. Indeed, in some regions, hunting can be a significant source of income for local communities and can also help to control certain populations of invasive animals.

In conclusion, banning hunting in areas affected by fires can have benefits for the conservation of wildlife, but it is important to consider the potential economic and social consequences of this measure.
