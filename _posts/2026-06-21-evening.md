### Drowning risks: what behaviors should you adopt to swim safely?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/c1b86366-6711-438c-b799-0ca58b377cbb/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Drowning risks: what behaviors should you adopt to swim safely?"
    caption="Risques de noyade : quels sont les comportements à adopter pour se baigner en toute sécurité ?" %}


In June, during the hot weather, many French people were enjoying swimming in various locations. To ensure safe and secure swimming, it is important to adopt certain behaviors.

For children, it is recommended to remain under adult supervision at all times. They should also wear a life jacket when in deep water or in the presence of currents. Adults should also wear a life jacket when engaging in dangerous water activities, such as deep-water swimming or scuba diving.

It is important not to drink alcohol before swimming, as it can impair judgment and coordination. It is also recommended to avoid jumping into the water from high places or venturing far from the swimming area.

It is important to respect warning signs and signs prohibiting swimming in swimming areas, as they may indicate dangerous areas or strong currents. It is also recommended not to swim in isolated areas where there is no immediate assistance in case of a problem.

Finally, it is important to know the signs of drowning and to know how to react in case of a problem. Signs of drowning may include erratic movements, loss of consciousness, or visible lack of breathing. In case of a problem, it is important to remain calm and not attempt to pull the person out of the water, as this can cause further drowning. It is recommended to call for help immediately.

By following these tips, it is possible to ensure safe and enjoyable swimming for everyone.
