### Heatwave: How to tell if your home is a thermal boiler during periods of extreme heat?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/afaf0ebf-cc44-46e5-91d9-fe18925865ba/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Heatwave: How to tell if your home is a thermal boiler during periods of extreme heat?"
    caption="Canicule : comment savoir si son logement est une bouilloire thermique en période de fortes chaleurs ?" %}


France is currently experiencing a heatwave, its third in less than two months. This period of high temperatures can make some homes stifling by trapping heat. To determine if your home is a "thermal boiler," HERE provides some guidance:

1. Windows: If your windows are closed and the temperature inside remains high, this may indicate that they are not allowing enough fresh air to enter.
2. Natural ventilation: If you do not have access to natural ventilation or if it is insufficient, this can contribute to the heat buildup in your home.
3. Building materials: Some materials, such as concrete and steel, can retain heat longer than others, such as wood or insulating materials.
4. Solar insolation: If your home is exposed to the sun, this can increase the indoor temperature and contribute to the heat buildup.
5. Air conditioning systems: If you do not have access to an air conditioning system or if it is insufficient, this can make your home hotter during heatwaves.
6. Regular maintenance: Regular maintenance of your home can help prevent heat from building up too quickly. This includes cleaning air conditioning filters, opening windows and doors to allow fresh air to circulate, and using fans or awnings.
7. Government measures: The French government has taken several measures to help people cope with the heat during heatwaves, including encouraging the use of energy-efficient lighting and providing advice on how to stay cool.

It is therefore important to consider these guidelines to prevent your home from becoming a "thermal boiler" during heatwaves.

### 'Risk of fire or explosion' in the event of propane leakage: heat pumps sold for five years recalled


Authorities have issued a recall for certain heat pumps sold over the past five years, due to a potential fire or explosion hazard if propane leaks. This measure is being taken after tests that revealed that some models may have a technical flaw, which could lead to a gas leak and thus a significant safety risk for users. Owners of the affected heat pumps are urged to contact their supplier or manufacturer immediately for information on the corrective measures to be taken, such as repairs or replacements of certain components. Authorities also recommend that users regularly check the condition of their heat pumps and carry out all necessary inspections and maintenance to ensure safety.
