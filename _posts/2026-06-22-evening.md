### International Trumpet Academy and Festival 🎺


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/fb05ff47-69f6-4e31-99c9-e2b506a3f235/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="International Trumpet Academy and Festival 🎺"
    caption="Académie et Festival International de Trompette 🎺" %}


The Academy and the International Trumpet Festival take place every year in Mulhouse, lasting for a week. The city is filled with the sounds of brass instruments. The events bring together professional and amateur musicians from all over the world to share their passion for the trumpet.

The Academy offers workshops, masterclasses, and lectures led by international experts. Participants have the opportunity to improve their trumpet skills and learn new techniques. The Festival provides musicians with the opportunity to perform on stage, both individually and in ensembles.

The concerts feature a variety of works, including classical, contemporary, and jazz pieces. Spectators can also attend instrument demonstrations, exhibitions, and lectures on the history of the trumpet.

The Academy and the International Trumpet Festival provide a unique opportunity for passionate trumpet players to meet, learn, and share their passion. They also contribute to enriching the cultural life of Mulhouse and promoting the city as an international center for the trumpet.

### Norway-Senegal: The Parisien's editorial predictions for the other Group I clash at the World Cup


Norway-Senegal: Predictions from the Parisien newspaper for the next match in Group I of the World Cup.

The match between Norway and Senegal, scheduled for November 24th as part of the Qatar World Cup, is considered one of the most interesting of the first round. The two teams have already faced each other in international competitions, with each team winning once.

Norway reached the semi-finals of Euro 2000 and finished third in the Nations League in 2019. They are led by coach Ståle Solbakken, who has already coached the team between 2004 and 2007. The Norwegians won their two opening qualifying matches for the World Cup against Gibraltar (8-0) and Northern Ireland (2-0).

Senegal reached the semi-finals of the Africa Cup of Nations in 2019 and finished second in the Nations League in 2021. They are led by coach Aliou Cissé, who was captain of the team during their victory at the CAN in 2002. The Senegalese won their two opening qualifying matches for the World Cup against Cape Verde (3-1) and Guinea-Bissau (2-0).

Predictions from the Parisien journalists give the advantage to Norway, but the Senegalese are considered a dangerous team. The match is expected to be a close and interesting one.
