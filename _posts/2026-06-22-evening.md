### Fish that feed vegetables is the principle of aquaponics, as explained by François Petitet.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/06825d6f-e0f1-4a58-bc5f-ea0408415cdf/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Fish that feed vegetables is the principle of aquaponics, as explained by François Petitet."
    caption="Des poissons qui nourissent des légumes c'est le principe de l'Aquaponie présenté par François Petitet" %}


Aquaponics is an agro-ecological system presented by François Petitet that involves combining the cultivation of fish and vegetables. The three main components of this system are water containing microorganisms, fish, and vegetable plants.

The water contains organic waste from the fish, which serves as food for the microorganisms. These microorganisms rapidly multiply in the water and produce a nutrient-rich substance called bio-media, which is then used to feed the plants.

The fish are raised in aquaponic tanks where they live in natural and healthy conditions. The plants are placed in hydroponic or inert substrate systems, which allow for regular irrigation with the nutrient solution produced by the microorganisms.

Aquaponics offers several advantages over other methods of fish and vegetable cultivation. It uses less water than conventional agriculture, as most of the water is recycled within the system. Furthermore, it allows for the production of fish and vegetables in an urban environment, which reduces transportation costs and greenhouse gas emissions.

Finally, aquaponics offers a sustainable and environmentally friendly way to produce food for human consumption, while also improving the quality of water and soil.
