### The King's Child's Caprices: When Molière, Cyrano, and d'Artagnan Meet


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/f3f15fcf-0aff-4c6f-b929-176350c8f7a6/920x517?webp=false"

Title: The King's Child's Caprices

Michel Leclerc writes a historical comedy titled "The King's Child's Caprices," which brings together the characters of Molière, Cyrano de Bergerac, d'Artagnan, and the young Louis XIV in a single adventure. This work depicts historical events around the reign of Louis XIV at a young age, where his whims and his desire to have fun are the subject of an exceptional spectacle.

The play takes place in a precise historical setting, with exceptional sets that allow the audience to immerse themselves in the atmosphere of the 17th century. Molière, Cyrano, and d'Artagnan are presented here as key figures of this era, who have a significant influence on the young king.

"The King's Child's Caprices" thus offers an interesting look at the court of Louis XIV, highlighting the complex relationships between power and art, as well as the rivalries and intrigues that marked this era. The play is an ambitious work that brings together a talented cast and offers the audience a high-quality spectacle.

### After the latest postponement of the opening of the 15th South line of the subway, everyone is expressing their 'frustration.'


The project for the southern section of Paris's Metro Line 15 has been postponed to an indefinite date. This decision has provoked a strong reaction from users and residents of the affected areas, who have expressed their frustration by shouting "enough!". The southern section of Line 15 was to connect the Orly station with the Montparnasse station, passing through the Val de Fontenay shopping center. The postponement of the project is due to technical and financial problems, particularly related to the high cost of construction and delays in the work. The authorities have promised to work to find a solution and to implement an alternative transport service during the postponement.

### Heatwave: More than 1,300 deaths attributable to the heatwave in Europe, according to the WHO


Heatwave in Europe: According to the World Health Organization (WHO), over 1,300 deaths have been attributed to the heatwave that struck Europe. This heatwave, characterized by high temperatures and intense sunshine, has had a particularly significant impact on the countries of northern Europe, where infrastructure and habitats are not adapted to extreme climatic conditions. Authorities have called for precautions to be taken to avoid serious consequences, particularly for vulnerable people, such as the elderly or those with chronic illnesses. Aid measures have also been put in place in some countries to help meet the needs of the population affected by the heatwave.

### After the storm and the heatwave, Paris is already saying goodbye to the Pont-Neuf cave, signed by JR.


Following the storm and the heatwave, the graffiti wall located under the New Bridge in Paris was removed. This work by the French artist JR, created in 2015, featured the faces of people from various Parisian communities. The decision to remove the wall sparked some controversy, with some arguing that it was necessary to maintain the cleanliness of the city, while others saw it as an example of the destruction of public art. The wall was replaced with a new stone wall, but it has not yet been announced whether JR or another artist will be responsible for repainting it.
