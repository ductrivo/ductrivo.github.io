    alt="In pictures. The gas station at Étoile-sur-Rhône, a small 'White House' that has withstood time and crises."
    caption="En images. La station-service d’Étoile-sur-Rhône, une petite “Maison blanche” qui a résisté au temps et aux crises" %}


The Étoile-sur-Rhône service station is a historic "White House," resilient through time and crises. Created in 1956 by Yvette and Raymond Ducol, it was located along the National 7. After 70 years, the two sons of the founders, Yves and Claude, continued to operate this rare place. Compared to the White House for its rotunda, the service station survived oil crises, the creation of the A7, as well as the diversion of the N7. For the past two years, it has housed a museum that recounts its most beautiful hours and brings back memories to nostalgic fans of old service stations. Guided tours are offered.

### Tons of concrete and asphalt destroyed: In Rennes, the Vilaine is now open-air to serve as a 'cool island'
