Prince Albert II of Monaco made a visit to lands of his ancestors in the commune of Drôme, France. On May 6, he visited the village of Bathernay, which is considered the cradle of the dynasty of the princes of Monaco, the Grimaldi. This visit took place within the framework of a memorial trail following the footsteps of his ancestors Imbert de Bathernay and Georgette de Montchenu. This visit marks an important event for the commune of Drôme and strengthens the links between the royal family of Monaco and its origin region.

### In pictures. Relive Prince Albert II of Monaco's visit to a village in the Drôme hills.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://cdn-s-www.ledauphine.com/images/AF94E8F5-32A3-444A-A620-0389EFDA96A9/NW_listB/revivez-la-visite-du-prince-albert-ii-de-monaco-dans-un-village-de-la-drome-des-collines-1778091814.jpg"
    alt="In pictures. Relive Prince Albert II of Monaco's visit to a village in the Drôme hills."
    caption="En images. Revivez la visite du prince Albert II de Monaco dans un village de la Drôme des collines" %}


Prince Albert II of Monaco made a commemorative visit to a hillside village in the Drôme, following the footsteps of his ancestor Imbert de Bathernay. He visited Bathernay, the fief of the powerful Renaissance lord.

During this visit, Albert II explored the ruins of the 15th-century Bathernay castle, which was the main residence of Imbert de Bathernay. He also visited Saint-Jean-Baptiste church where his ancestor is buried.

The prince was welcomed by local representatives and discussed the preservation of historical sites with regional cultural heritage officials. He also met villagers and exchanged with them about their daily life and relationship to their land.

Finally, Albert II visited a local museum dedicated to the history of the Drôme des Collines, where he learned more about the historical events that marked the village and its region. He also had the opportunity to taste local culinary specialties such as cream ravioli and escargots à la bourride.

This visit allowed Prince Albert II of Monaco to strengthen his links with his past and bring particular attention to the historical sites of his family. It was also an opportunity for the villagers of Bathernay to share their cultural heritage with a prestigious visitor.

### Emirates: the annual profits of the airline company are increasing despite the war in the Middle East


Emirates, the Emirati airline, recorded an annual profit increase despite the war in the Middle East. The financial figures for 2021 were recently published, showing a net profit of 3.7 billion euros, an over 50% increase compared to the previous year. This performance is due to revenue growth and effective cost management. Emirates transported nearly 13 million passengers in 2021, with an average load factor of 75%. The airline also reduced ticket-related losses thanks to increased prices and better reservation management. Emirates also invested in fleet modernization in 2021, with the purchase of new Boeing 787-9 Dreamliner and Airbus A380 aircraft. However, the outlook for 2022 remains uncertain due to Covid-19 pandemic restrictions and political conflicts in the Middle East region.
