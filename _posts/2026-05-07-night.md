### Valley of the Drôme. Will the rosé of Clarette de Die return soon?


{% include figure.html
    src="https://cdn-s-www.ledauphine.com/images/3945F75D-E7BA-41CF-BC32-5852EEB86F2A/NW_listB/la-clairette-de-die-rose-sera-t-elle-bientot-de-retour-1778167521.jpg"
    alt="Valley of the Drôme. Will the rosé of Clarette de Die return soon?"
    caption="Vallée de la Drôme. La clairette de Die rosé sera-t-elle bientôt de retour ?" %}


The Valley of the Drôme announces the imminent return of the rosé de Clarette de Die. After a decade of procedure, the producers' syndicate must deposit a new charter at the Inao (National Institute for Origin and Quality) to obtain the Controlled Designation of Origin. The rosé de Clarette de Die was removed from the market in 2011, following an Inao decision that considered the vinegrowers were not adhering to production rules. However, since then, efforts have been made to reform the charter and ensure better quality of the product. If the demand is accepted by the Inao, the rosé de Clarette de Die could return to shelves in 2023.

*[Source](https://www.ledauphine.com/economie/2026/05/07/bientot-le-retour-de-la-clairette-rose)*

### Air pollution in the metro of Paris: justice rules against RATP and validates an air analysis requested by drivers.


The court has rejected the RATP's position by validating an air analysis requested by Paris Metro drivers, who accused their company of exposing passengers and employees to high levels of pollution. The court's decision was made after evidence showed a higher concentration of fine particles in certain Metro wagons compared to standards set by the French Agency for Health and Environmental Safety (AFSSA). The drivers had also reported symptoms such as respiratory pain and asthma problems. The RATP was invited to take measures to reduce pollution in the Metro wagons, including improving air filtration and implementing a more effective ventilation system.
