### Origin, transmission, symptoms: what is known about the hantavirus that caused three deaths on a cruise ship.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/07fbfb87-eecb-4ff8-999f-b1a86c8ec584/140x140_sc_000-a9r86e6.jpg"
    alt="Origin, transmission, symptoms: what is known about the hantavirus that caused three deaths on a cruise ship."
    caption="Origine, transmission, symptômes : ce que l'on sait de l'hantavirus qui a fait trois morts sur un navire de croisière" %}


A hantavirus has been identified on a cruise ship linking Ushuaia, Argentina, to the Cape Verde archipelago, causing the death of three passengers and suspecting five other cases. The hantavirus is a family of viruses transmitted by rodents or their urine. Symptoms include abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, muscle pains, and acute kidney failure. The virus can be fatal in about 30-40% of cases. Hantavirus is endemic in South America, Asia, and Europe. There is no vaccine against hantavirus, but preventive measures include avoiding rodents and disinfecting areas contaminated by their urine. Health authorities are investigating the possible outbreak on the cruise ship to identify transmission sources and take appropriate measures to protect passengers and crew.

### Origin, transmission, symptoms: what is known about the hantavirus that caused three deaths on a cruise ship.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/05/07fbfb87-eecb-4ff8-999f-b1a86c8ec584/140x140_sc_000-a9r86e6.jpg"

    caption="Origine, transmission, symptômes : ce que l'on sait de l'hantavirus qui a fait trois morts sur un navire de croisière" %}


A hanatavirus has been identified on a cruise ship linking Ushuaia, Argentina, to the Cape Verde archipelago, causing the death of three passengers and suspecting five other cases. The hanatavirus is a virus transmitted by rodents or their urine, which can be contracted by inhaling contaminated particles. There are more than 70 types of hanataviruses in the world, some of which are fatal to humans. Symptoms include abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and acute kidney failure. The hanatavirus is not contagious between humans, but it can be transmitted to other people who are exposed to the urine or feces of infected rodents. Prevention measures include avoiding areas where rodents are present and regular disinfection of public spaces. The investigation is ongoing to determine if the cruise ship was a source of hanatavirus infection.
