### Pigs in front of a mosque in Isère: Former FN official receives 10 months in prison with suspended sentence for inciting hatred


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/pikapi/images/74d5acc5-4bdf-44b9-9722-6409aede71d5/920x517?webp=false"
    alt="Pigs in front of a mosque in Isère: Former FN official receives 10 months in prison with suspended sentence for inciting hatred"
    caption="Os de porc devant une mosquée en Isère : un ex-élu FN écope de 10 mois de prison avec sursis pour provocation à la haine" %}


On June 10, the far-right former elected official, Franck Sinisi, was sentenced to 10 months in prison with a suspended sentence by a French court for inciting hatred. The charges against Sinisi stemmed from events that occurred in September of the previous year in Fontaine, in the Isère department. He vandalized a Muslim prayer hall and left the remains of pork in front of it. These actions were considered to be a racial hate provocation against the local Muslim community. Sinisi was a member of the National Front (FN), a French far-right political party, before being elected mayor of Fontaine in 2014. He was removed from his position in December 2019 for administrative reasons.

### 'Paris is only the visible tip of the iceberg': Agnès Evren will lead the Senate's inquiry commission into after-school care.

This investigation aims to examine the inequalities and problems within the French education system, particularly in Paris. The goal is to propose solutions to improve the quality of education and reduce disparities between schools in different regions of the country.
