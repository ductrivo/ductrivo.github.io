In 1920, marked by a post-war "expensive life," strict regulations were put in place to control the weight, size, and price of the baguette. These measures were taken to reduce social tensions during a difficult period. After World War I, the prices of basic goods had increased significantly, leading to a large disparity between social classes. Therefore, the authorities decided to impose standards on bread, an essential product in daily life, in order to limit these inequalities and maintain social balance.

Local authorities have also taken initiatives to improve the situation. For example, they have created a regulated motorcycle tourism route in order to attract motorcyclists to areas more suitable for their activity while reducing noise pollution for local residents.
