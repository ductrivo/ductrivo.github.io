### 'The employer has the responsibility to encourage its employees to take time off,' Anne Leleu-Eté, labor law attorney


According to Anne Leleu-Eté, an employment law attorney, employers are obligated to encourage their employees to take time off. This responsibility implies that companies must encourage their staff to take breaks to maintain good mental and physical health, as well as high productivity at work.

This obligation can be fulfilled in several ways, such as by implementing a system of paid or unpaid leave, encouraging employees to take holidays and annual leave, and promoting mental and physical health in the workplace.

In addition, companies must also comply with the laws and regulations governing leave, including minimum annual leave requirements and conditions for accessing paid leave.

It is important to note that this employer responsibility regarding leave is not universal and varies depending on the country and local legislation.
