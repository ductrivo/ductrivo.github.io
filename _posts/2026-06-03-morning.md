### TV Audiences: 'Appeal for witnesses' on Xavier Dupont de Ligonnès behind 'Koh Lanta'


The television program "Call for Witnesses" aired on the French channel "Koh Lanta" on February 18, 2023. The show called on potential witnesses to apply to provide information about the Xavier Dupont de Ligonnès case, a notorious criminal case in France that occurred in 2011.

Xavier Dupont de Ligonnès is accused of murdering his wife, his four children, and his father, and then hiding their bodies in their house in Nantes. He was convicted in the first instance for these murders in 2014, but the Court of Cassation acquitted him in 2018.

The "Call for Witnesses" show announced that it was seeking additional information about the case, including testimonies that could provide new evidence or renew public interest in this case. Potential witnesses were invited to contact the channel directly to provide confidential information.

This show has attracted a great deal of media attention in France, with many comments on social media and in traditional media. Some have criticized the show for its revisiting of the case, while others have praised it for continuing to draw attention to a criminal case that has marked French society.

The "Call for Witnesses" show is a French television program that has been airing since 2013, in which confidential testimonies are presented to solve unsolved crimes or reactivate old cases.

### India: At least 21 dead and more than 40 injured in New Delhi building fire


A fire broke out in a building in New Delhi, India, resulting in at least 21 deaths and more than 40 injuries. Authorities said the fire started in the morning of December 13th and quickly escalated. The victims were primarily migrant workers who were sleeping in the building, which did not have a residential permit. The fire was considered a major disaster for the community of migrant workers in New Delhi. Authorities have launched an investigation into the circumstances of the fire and have promised to improve safety conditions in the city's residential buildings.

### 'There are still messages from inmates in the cell': the former prison of Troyes is decorated with the first escape game


The former prison of Troyes, located in France, is being transformed into an escape game center. There are still messages written by inmates on the prison walls, which testify to their experience and life within this institution. The prison closed in 2013 and opened to the public as an escape game center in 2018. Visitors can participate in escape games that simulate different detention scenarios, such as escaping from a prison or escaping from a secret laboratory. The escape game center was created by a French company specializing in escape games and has received positive feedback from visitors. The messages written by the inmates are preserved as a historical record of the prisoners' experience in this institution.
