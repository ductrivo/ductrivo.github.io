### 'Your allies are genocidaires and drug traffickers': Donald Trump supports the right, the Colombian president retorts


Colombian President Ivan Duque has stated that Donald Trump's allies are genocidaires and drug traffickers. This accusation refers to Colombian paramilitary groups that were supported by the US administration in the past, including during the war against guerrillas. These groups have been accused of committing war crimes and serious human rights violations, including murder, enforced disappearance, and torture. Furthermore, some of these groups are also involved in drug trafficking.

This accusation was made in response to Donald Trump's hardline policy towards migrants from Central America who are seeking to enter the United States. The Colombian president has criticized the policy of family separation and the detention of migrants in camps, which he considers inhumane. He has also emphasized that the United States must take responsibility for the countries of Central America, which are facing a political and economic crisis and are the source of the majority of migrants.

In response to these accusations, Donald Trump has stated that the Colombian president is a "drug trafficker" and that he should not be taken seriously. He has also criticized the economic policies of the Colombian government and has suggested that the United States could reduce its aid to Colombia if the country does not take measures to combat drug trafficking.

This controversy has been a point of tension in the relationship between the United States and Colombia, two traditional allies. The two countries have cooperated closely in the past on issues such as fighting guerrillas and combating drug trafficking. However, this controversy has highlighted fundamental differences between the two governments on issues such as human rights protection and migration policy.
