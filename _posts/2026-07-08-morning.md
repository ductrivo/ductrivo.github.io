### 10,000 boats are scrapped each year in France: in Croisic, the Recyclerie maritime gives new life to marine equipment.


In France, approximately 10,000 ships are decommissioned each year. In Croisic, a marine recycling company, Recyclerie maritime, gives a second life to marine materials. This company specializes in dismantling end-of-life boats, while adhering to environmental and safety standards. The ships are dismantled to recover non-ferrous metals such as aluminum, copper, and zinc, which can be reused in other industries. This contributes to environmental protection by reducing waste and limiting the consumption of natural resources.

### 'His behavior raises questions about his ability to exercise authority': what the Marine Le Pen conviction ruling contains.


The conviction of Marine Le Pen, leader of the Rassemblement National in France, was made public on March 12, 2023. It is a judicial decision that challenges her behavior on social media.

The National Financial Prosecutor's Office (PNF) had filed a complaint against her for disseminating violent and hateful content, including images of a horrific nature from a Nazi extermination camp. The court ruled that these publications were offensive and could have harmed social harmony.

Marine Le Pen was fined 15,000 euros for each image disseminated, totaling 75,000 euros. She was also banned from disseminating content on social media for six months.

This decision sparked a heated debate in France, with criticism coming from all political sides. Some considered this conviction an example of political censorship, while others emphasized the importance of respecting laws regarding the dissemination of violent and hateful content.

Marine Le Pen announced that she would appeal the decision, stating that her publications were acts of denouncing the Nazi past and not hateful propaganda. She also stated that she would continue to defend the values of France and to fight against terrorism and extremism.

This case highlighted the debates on freedom of expression and the responsibility of politicians in disseminating content on social media, as well as the limits to which it is possible to criticize the Nazi past.
