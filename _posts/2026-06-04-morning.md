### Marjane Satrapi, the author of the comic book series 'Persepolis' and filmmaker, died at the age of 56.


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/06/ba6e45a1-e5ca-4c29-bb97-2dccecf37ed0/140x140_sc_satrapi.jpg"
    alt="Marjane Satrapi, the author of the comic book series 'Persepolis' and filmmaker, died at the age of 56."
    caption="Marjane Satrapi, autrice de la série de la bande dessinée 'Persepolis' et réalisatrice, est morte à l'âge de 56 ans" %}


Marjane Satrapi, the author of the iconic comic book series "Persepolis" and filmmaker, died last Thursday at the age of 56. The Franco-Iranian artist, known for her autobiographical work that tells the story of her life during the Iranian Revolution and the Gulf Wars, was announced dead to AFP by her associates. Her death caused a great stir in the world of comics and more broadly in the international cultural scene. Marjane Satrapi is considered one of the great contemporary comic book authors, having won numerous awards for her work, including the Prix du livre inter and the Renaudot Prize. She also directed several animated films based on her comics, including "Persepolis" in 2007, which was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature. Marjane Satrapi leaves behind a significant legacy in the world of comics and literature, with works that have touched readers with their sincerity and strength.

### Xavier Dupont de Ligonnès case: The Arcom 'will examine the sequence' broadcast on M6, confirms its president


The Xavier Dupont de Ligonnès case is still under investigation. The president of the Autorité de régulation des communications (ARCOM) has confirmed that his commission will examine the broadcast on M6, which deals with this sensitive and controversial case.

Xavier Dupont de Ligonnès is accused of having murdered his wife, his four children, and his father in 2011. He was sentenced to life imprisonment by a French court in 2014.

The television sequence in question is a documentary titled "L'Affaire Dupont de Ligonnès: the killer's secret," broadcast on M6 in March 2023. It has sparked a heated controversy, as it was produced without the consent of the victims' families and without the participation of the police or the justice system.

ARCOM is responsible for regulating audiovisual media in France and ensuring that broadcasts respect the rights of individuals and the law. The commission will therefore examine whether the documentary violated these rules, particularly in relation to the invasion of the privacy of the victims and their families, as well as the use of confidential images or information.

If ARCOM finds that the rules have been violated, it can sanction M6 by imposing a fine or prohibiting it from broadcasting certain programs for a certain period.
