{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/06/210d4692-1f4a-4d7e-b881-5b3936a26dba/464x261_sc_capucine-viglione-4-jean-marie-hervio-afp.jpg"

    caption="'Démocratiser les corps féminins musclés' : la prise de parole de la grimpeuse Capucine Viglione" %}


Title: Capucine Viglione's Speech on Democratizing the Bodies of Muscled Female Climbers

Capucine Viglione, a member of the French climbing team, has chosen to speak publicly to share the inappropriate comments she has received over several years. Her main goal is to protect young girls who are starting the sport.

She highlighted that women with muscular bodies are often stigmatized and considered unfeminine, which creates pressure on female athletes in their field. She also mentioned the importance of positive representation of women in sports media and the need for greater visibility of muscular women.

Capucine Viglione also called for better understanding and a change in mindset so that women are accepted in their physical form, regardless of their size or musculature. She emphasized the importance of encouraging young girls to feel confident in their own bodies and not to be intimidated by stereotypes.

Finally, she called on sports officials to take measures to protect female athletes from inappropriate comments and to promote greater gender equality in sports.

### '1 or 2 million would really help us': A municipality in the Puy-de-Dôme region is appealing for aid for its damaged church.


The municipality of Saint-Nectaire, located in the Puy-de-Dôme department in France, is seeking help to repair its old church, which is threatened by significant cracks. The building dates back to the 12th century and has been classified as a historical monument since 1926. The estimated cost of the repair is around 1.5 million euros. The municipality has already spent more than 300,000 euros on preliminary work, but the cracks continue to spread. Local authorities have requested the help of the government and public donations to save the church.

### 'An interpretation, not a reproduction': a missing scene from the Bayeux tapestry created in the Dordogne


"An interpretation rather than a reproduction": a missing scene from the Bayeux tapestry has been created in the Creuse region. This new scene, titled "The Battle of Val-ès-Dunes", depicts a historical event that is not represented in the original medieval tapestry. It was designed by local artists to commemorate the 1047 battle between William the Conqueror and the rebellious Normans, which took place in the Creuse region. The tapestry is exhibited at the Guéret museum in Creuse.
