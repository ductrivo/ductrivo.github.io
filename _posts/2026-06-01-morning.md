### Charleville-Mézières: How the city was designed to compete with Paris


{% include figure.html
    src="https://www.ici.fr/s3/cruiser-production-eu3/2026/06/e868ce51-4064-4239-9139-88a7f93bd9d8/464x261_sc_gettyimages-955803290.jpg"
    alt="Charleville-Mézières: How the city was designed to compete with Paris"
    caption="Charleville-Mézières : comment la ville a été pensée pour rivaliser avec Paris" %}


Charleville-Mézières is a French city built in the early 17th century with the aim of competing with Paris. A prince decided to build an entire city to achieve this ambition. The city was designed with red bricks and elegant arcades, as well as a slightly larger square than that of Paris. Stéphane Bern tells us about the construction of Charleville-Mézières, which was built to be an important cultural and economic center in the region. The city was designed with meticulous planning, with regular streets and well-organized green spaces. It was also designed to accommodate a large garrison, which contributed to its economic development. Today, Charleville-Mézières is known for its museums, theaters, and vibrant culture. It remains an example of 17th-century architecture in France.

### 'The Fed is facing a test of resilience': the warning from Jerome Powell, former chairman of the US Federal Reserve.


The Fed (Federal Reserve Bank) is currently facing a major challenge, according to the former chairman of the U.S. Federal Reserve, Jerome Powell.

The situation is characterized by rising interest rates to combat inflation, which has reached a 40-year high in the United States. This measure is intended to slow economic growth and lower the prices of financial assets, particularly stocks and bonds.

However, rising interest rates also risk triggering an economic recession by curbing investment and consumption. Furthermore, it could lead to higher unemployment and lower incomes for U.S. households.

To mitigate these negative effects, the Fed must strike a balance between fighting inflation and preserving economic growth. This involves a cautious management of interest rates and close monitoring of financial markets.

Finally, the Fed must also consider the effects of its decisions on other countries, as inflation in the United States can have international repercussions. Therefore, it is important that the Fed work in collaboration with foreign central banks to maintain the stability of the global financial system.
